Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 1: Purpose
1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to ensure that all incidents, accidents, near-misses, and
safeguarding concerns are identified, recorded, reported, and managed in a timely, consistent, and
transparent manner.
This process protects the health, safety, and rights of all residents, staff, and visitors, and ensures
the organisation complies with the Health Act 2007, HIQA regulations, and HSE National Incident
Management Framework.
It also ensures:
- That learning is captured to prevent recurrence
- That open disclosure is practiced in line with ethical standards
- That incidents are used to drive service improvement and risk reduction

=====Incident_Reporting_Procedure_Section2_Scope.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 2: Scope
2. Scope
This procedure applies to all individuals involved with Secure Living Support Services Ltd, including:
- Full-time, part-time, relief, agency, and volunteer staff
- Residents receiving care or support services
- Family members or visitors on the premises
- Contractors, maintenance personnel, and third-party professionals
It covers all incidents occurring:
- On the premises, including residential units and communal areas
- During staff duties, including off-site support and transportation
- Online or via communication platforms, where abuse, threats, or data breaches may occur
- Involving property, personal injury, medication, or health and safety risks
This scope also includes near-misses, which, while not resulting in harm, pose a significant potential
risk to safety or wellbeing and must also be reported.

=====Incident_Reporting_Procedure_Section3_Definition_of_an_Incident.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 3: Definition of an Incident
3. Definition of an Incident
An incident is defined as any event or circumstance that:
- Has caused harm, injury, or distress to a resident, staff member, visitor, or contractor
- Had the potential to cause harm (near-miss)
- Interferes with the safety, care, or operations of the service
Examples of Reportable Incidents:
- Injuries: slips, falls, self-harm, assaults
- Aggression: verbal abuse, physical aggression, threats
- Medication incidents: missed doses, incorrect administration, or drug errors
- Safeguarding issues: abuse, neglect, exploitation
- Environmental risks: fires, floods, electrical faults, unsafe infrastructure
- Health and safety breaches: broken equipment, unsafe procedures, hazards
- Data breaches: lost records, unauthorised access to personal data
- Missing persons: absconsion, residents not returning from leave
- Emergency service call-outs: Gardaí, fire brigade, ambulance
- Property damage or theft
All staff must treat near-misses with the same urgency as actual harm events to prevent future risks.

=====Incident_Reporting_Procedure_Section4_Reporting_Responsibilities.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 4: Reporting Responsibilities
4. Reporting Responsibilities
Secure Living Support Services Ltd requires that all staff, regardless of role or seniority, understand
and fulfil their duty to report incidents promptly and accurately.
4.1 General Staff Duties
- All staff must report any incident or near-miss immediately to their line manager or the Person in
Charge (PIC).
- Incident reporting is a non-punitive process - staff will not be penalised for reporting in good faith.
- The first staff member who becomes aware of the incident is responsible for ensuring the report is
submitted.
4.2 Role of the Person in Charge (PIC)
- Ensures that the Incident Report Form is completed within one hour of the incident.
- Initiates immediate safety actions if required (e.g. medical attention, Garda call-out).
- Notifies senior management and other agencies (e.g. HIQA, HSE, Gardaí) if the incident meets the
criteria for mandatory notification.
- Records the event in the Incident Log Register and ensures follow-up actions are taken.
4.3 Escalation Responsibilities
- If an incident involves potential abuse, the Designated Safeguarding Officer (DSO) must be
informed.
- The DSO will initiate a safeguarding response under the HSE Safeguarding Vulnerable Persons
Policy.
- For serious incidents, the Registered Provider must be notified and may take over case
management.

=====Incident_Reporting_Procedure_Section5_Reporting_Process.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 5: Reporting Process
5. Reporting Process
To ensure swift and accurate handling of all incidents, staff must follow this 5-step reporting process:
Step 1: Ensure Immediate Safety
- Provide first aid or medical help if needed
- Remove others from danger or distress
- Alert emergency services (Gardaí, ambulance, fire) if the situation requires it
Step 2: Notify the Person in Charge (PIC)
- Report the incident to the most senior staff on duty without delay
- If the incident involves suspected abuse, also notify the Designated Safeguarding Officer (DSO)
Step 3: Complete the Incident Report Form
- Fill out the form within one hour of the incident
- Include date, time, location, people involved, witnesses, and actions taken
- Be factual and objective - avoid opinions or assumptions
Step 4: PIC Logs and Reviews
- The PIC checks and signs the report
- The incident is logged in the Incident Log Register
- The PIC determines whether external notifications are required (e.g. HIQA, HSE, Gardaí)
Step 5: Escalation and Debrief
- If serious, escalate to senior management and notify family/next of kin
- A debrief is arranged with staff and/or residents involved
- A risk review or service improvement may be initiated based on findings

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 6: Documentation
6. Documentation
Accurate and timely documentation is essential to ensure transparency, legal compliance, and
continuous improvement in safeguarding practices.
6.1 Incident Report Form
- Every incident must be recorded using the Incident Report Form within 1 hour of the event.
- The form must include:
  - Date and time of incident
  - Names of all individuals involved or affected
  - Location and description of what occurred
  - Immediate actions taken
  - Name and signature of the reporting staff member and reviewing PIC
6.2 Incident Log Register
- All reports are logged in a central Incident Log Register maintained by the PIC or manager.
- The register tracks incident trends, frequency, and outcomes of investigations.
- It supports audit readiness and management oversight.
6.3 Notification Records
- Copies of any external notifications (e.g. to HIQA, HSE, Gardaí) must be retained on file.
- Evidence of submission via portals, emails, or recorded phone calls should be attached to the
incident file.
6.4 Resident File Entries
- If a resident is involved, a brief factual entry is made in their individual care record, referencing the
incident and any follow-up support.

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 7: Notification
7. Notification
Timely notification of relevant internal and external parties is critical to ensure transparency,
accountability, and compliance with legal obligations.
7.1 Internal Notifications
- The Person in Charge (PIC) is notified immediately for all incidents.
- The Designated Safeguarding Officer (DSO) is informed if any abuse or protection concern is
suspected.
- The Registered Provider is informed of all serious incidents or patterns of concern.
7.2 External Notifications
Depending on the nature and severity of the incident, the following agencies may need to be
notified:
- HIQA: Within 3 working days (or immediately if serious) - death, serious injury, allegation of abuse
- HSE Safeguarding Team: Immediately if abuse suspected - physical, financial, emotional, neglect
- Gardaí: Immediately if a crime is suspected - assault, theft, missing person
- Tusla (Child & Family Agency): If a child is involved - allegations or concerns involving minors
- Health & Safety Authority (HSA): Within 10 days if notifiable - dangerous occurrence, major
accident
- Next of Kin: As soon as practical - resident involved in incident or emergency
7.3 Evidence of Notification
- Copies of notifications, emails, call records, or online submission receipts must be retained and
stored securely with the incident report.
- Notifications must be clearly recorded in the Incident Log Register with the date and staff
responsible.

=====Incident_Reporting_Procedure_Section8_Debriefing_and_Learning.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 8: Debriefing and Learning
8. Debriefing and Learning
Following each significant incident, Secure Living Support Services Ltd ensures a structured
debriefing process and promotes a culture of continuous learning.
8.1 Staff and Resident Debrief
- Affected staff and/or residents are invited to participate in a supportive debrief after the incident.
- The debrief focuses on:
  - Emotional impact
  - Clarifying the facts of what happened
  - Identifying what worked well and what could improve
- The Person in Charge (PIC) facilitates the debrief or assigns a qualified staff member.
8.2 Learning Reviews
- For serious or repeat incidents, the management team may carry out a Learning Review to
examine:
  - Root causes of the incident
  - Gaps in procedure, communication, or environment
  - Opportunities to improve safety and care
- These reviews may include input from frontline staff, clinical leads, or external advisors.
8.3 Sharing and Training
- Key lessons learned are shared at:
  - Monthly Governance & Risk Management meetings
  - Staff briefings and supervision sessions
  - Annual training updates where policies or procedures are revised
- Where necessary, staff receive additional targeted training.
8.4 Policy and Practice Improvement
- Learning outcomes may trigger:
  - Updates to this Incident Reporting Procedure
  - New risk controls or environmental changes
  - Updates to induction training for new staff

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 9: Record Retention
9. Record Retention
All records relating to incidents must be securely stored, readily accessible for inspection, and
retained in line with legal and regulatory requirements.
9.1 Storage and Security
- Incident reports, logs, and related documents must be stored securely in a locked cabinet or
secure digital system with restricted access.
- Only authorised personnel (e.g. PIC, Registered Provider, Designated Safeguarding Officer) may
access these records.
9.2 Retention Period
- All incident records must be retained for a minimum of 7 years after the closure of the case, or
longer if specified by legal or regulatory requirements.
- If the incident involves a minor, records must be kept until the person reaches age 25.
9.3 Inspection and Review
- Records must be available to HIQA, HSE, or other regulatory bodies upon request.
- Internal audits of incident records will be conducted quarterly by the Registered Provider or
designated quality officer to ensure completeness, accuracy, and compliance.
9.4 Destruction of Records
- After the appropriate retention period, records must be destroyed securely (e.g. shredding physical
copies or deleting encrypted digital files).
- A record of destroyed files must be maintained.

=====Incident_Reporting_Procedure_Section10_Policy_Review_and_Audit.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Incident Reporting Procedure - Section 10: Policy Review and Audit
10. Policy Review and Audit
This policy is a living document and must be reviewed regularly to ensure it reflects current
legislation, best practice, and organisational learning.
10.1 Scheduled Review
- This procedure will be reviewed at least annually by the Registered Provider and Person in Charge
(PIC).
- Interim reviews will be carried out in response to:
  - Changes in legislation or HIQA standards
  - A serious incident that reveals policy or procedural gaps
  - Recommendations from an audit, inspection, or safeguarding review
10.2 Audit and Monitoring
- The implementation of this policy is monitored through:
  - Quarterly audits of the Incident Log and individual reports
  - Monthly management meetings where trends are analysed
  - Feedback from staff debriefs and supervision
- Outcomes from audits are documented and actions tracked until resolved.
10.3 Staff Feedback
- All staff are encouraged to suggest improvements to the reporting process or this policy.
- Suggestions are reviewed by the PIC or management team and, where appropriate, incorporated
into the next policy update.
