Child Safeguarding Statement

Risk Assessment & Policy Document

Chosanta Management is committed to safeguarding children and young persons in accordance with the Children First Act 2015, the Children First: National Guidance for the Protection and Welfare of Children (2017), and Tusla's Child Safeguarding: A Guide for Policy, Procedure and Practice.

Service Provided: Chosanta Management provides accommodation coordination and property management services. While our services are not primarily directed at children, we recognise that children and young persons may reside in or visit properties managed or coordinated through our network.

2 Nature of Service & Principles

Chosanta Management has carried out a risk assessment of any potential for harm to a child while availing of our services. Below is a list of the areas of risk identified and the procedures in place to manage those risks.

Guiding Principles: The safety and welfare of children is everyone's responsibility. Chosanta Management will ensure, as far as practicable, that children are protected from harm while utilising, residing in, or visiting properties or services coordinated through our organisation.

Identified Risks & Mitigation Procedures

1 A child could be harmed by a member of staff or volunteer All personnel are vetted. Code of conduct in place. Safe recruitment practices followed. Low

3 A child could be harmed due to inadequate supervision at a property All accommodation providers are required to have appropriate supervision. Properties inspected regularly. Low–Medium

Procedures & Declaration

Safeguarding Procedures & Sign-off

4 Procedures in Place
  • Procedure for reporting child protection or welfare concerns to Tusla
  • Procedure for maintaining a list of mandated persons under the Children First Act 2015
  • Procedure for appointing a relevant person under the Children First Act 2015
  • Safe recruitment procedures including Garda vetting for all staff and contractors
  • Procedure for managing and recording allegations of abuse or misconduct
  • Procedure for provision of information and training to staff on child safeguarding
  • Procedure for reporting concerns to Tusla — using standard reporting form
  • Procedure for maintaining confidentiality in line with GDPR and data protection
  • Code of conduct for all personnel interacting with children or families
  • Anti-bullying procedures for any accommodation settings where children may be present
5 Implementation & Review

This Child Safeguarding Statement has been developed in line with the requirements of the Children First Act 2015 and Tusla's Guidance. It will be reviewed every 24 months or as soon as practicable after a material change in any matter to which it refers.

6 Declaration

This Child Safeguarding Statement was adopted by Chosanta Management on the date set out below. We confirm that we have carried out a risk assessment as required under the Children First Act 2015 and that this statement is published in accordance with our legal obligations.
