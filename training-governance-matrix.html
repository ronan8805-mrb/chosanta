Training Governance & Compliance Matrix

Section 1: Purpose, Scope & Mandatory Training

1 Purpose

This document establishes Chosanta Management's training governance framework. It is grounded in the following legislative and regulatory requirements:

  • Child Care (Standards in Children's Residential Centres) Regulations 2018 (S.I. No. 674/2017), Regulation 16 — Staff Training and Development: The registered provider must ensure all staff have access to appropriate training to carry out their duties competently; training records must be maintained and available for inspection
  • HIQA National Standards for Children's Residential Centres 2018, Standard 6 (Responsive Workforce): Providers must plan, organise, and manage the workforce; ensure staff have required qualifications, skills and competencies; provide effective induction for new staff; and support ongoing professional development
  • National Vetting Bureau (Children and Vulnerable Persons) Acts 2012–2016: Mandatory Garda vetting before commencement of relevant work; Tusla-regulated services require re-vetting every 3 years; vetting is non-transferable between organisations
  • Children First Act 2015: All staff are mandated persons; Children First training is a statutory requirement and must be completed at induction and refreshed every 2 years
  • Child Care (Placement of Children in Residential Care) Regulations 1995 (S.I. 259/1995): Training records, qualifications, and personnel files must be available at inspection
2 Scope
  • All staff — full-time, part-time, relief, agency, volunteers, and student placements
  • Management and supervisory staff responsible for training oversight
  • The PIC and Governance Officer responsible for regulatory training compliance
  • Contracted professionals with direct service user contact

Section 3: Competency Sign-Off & Training Schedule

6 Competency Sign-Off Process

Attending a training course alone does not confirm competency. Chosanta Management requires a competency sign-off process to ensure that training translates into safe, effective practice:

  • Observation: Following training, the staff member is observed performing the relevant skill in practice by a senior staff member or PIC
  • Assessment: A competency assessment form is completed — confirming the staff member demonstrates the required knowledge and skill to the expected standard
  • Sign-off: Both the staff member and assessor sign the competency record — this is filed in the personnel record and the training tracker updated
  • Re-assessment: Where a staff member does not meet the required standard, additional training, mentoring, or supervised practice is provided before reassessment
  • Critical competencies: Medication administration, manual handling, and fire warden duties require competency sign-off before independent practice is permitted

8 Training Records & Evidence
  • Training certificates must be obtained for all external and certified courses — originals or verified copies filed in personnel records
  • Internal training attendance is recorded using sign-in sheets — retained with session content notes and facilitator details
  • The training register is maintained centrally and is available for HIQA/Tusla inspection at all times
  • Staff are responsible for notifying the Governance Officer of any training completed externally (e.g. through previous employers or personal development)
  • Training compliance is reported as a standing agenda item at monthly governance meetings

Section 4: Governance Oversight & Individual Training Record

9 Training Governance Oversight
  • Monthly review: The Governance Officer reviews the training tracker monthly, identifies expiring certifications, and arranges refresher training proactively
  • Quarterly reporting: A training compliance summary is presented at the quarterly governance review meeting — including compliance rates, gaps, and planned actions
  • Annual training needs analysis: A formal assessment of organisational training needs conducted annually — informed by incident trends, inspection findings, legislative changes, and staff appraisal outcomes
  • Budget allocation: An annual training budget is approved by the Director — covering external courses, certification renewals, and CPD opportunities
  • Inspection readiness: The training register, certificates folder, and compliance tracker must be available at all times for regulatory inspection
