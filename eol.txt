1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that every resident of Secure Living Support Services Ltd
receives compassionate, dignified, and person-centred care as they approach the end of life. Our
commitment is to:
- Promote comfort, respect, and quality of life in the final stages of life.
- Honour the cultural, spiritual, and personal values and wishes of each resident.
- Support residents, their families, and significant others with sensitivity and empathy.
- Provide a structured framework for staff to deliver consistent and high-quality end-of-life care.
- Ensure compliance with national standards, ethical principles, and relevant legislation, including
the Health Act 2007 and HIQA's National Standards for Residential Services for Children and Adults
with Disabilities.
2. Scope
This policy applies to all staff, healthcare professionals, volunteers, and associated care partners
involved in the care of residents approaching the end of life within Secure Living Support Services
Ltd.
3. Principles
- Respect for the dignity, rights, and wishes of each resident.
- Holistic and individualised care planning.
- Effective communication and collaboration with residents, families, and healthcare teams.
- Provision of physical, emotional, psychological, spiritual, and social support.
- Advance care planning and early identification of end-of-life needs.
- Compliance with legal and ethical standards, including the Assisted Decision-Making (Capacity)
Act 2015.
4. Responsibilities
- Manager/Person in Charge (PIC): Ensures policy implementation, staff training, and coordination
with palliative care teams.
- Care Staff: Deliver compassionate care and promptly report changes in condition.
- Clinical Staff: Develop and review end-of-life care plans, administer pain relief, and liaise with
palliative teams.
- All Staff: Respect confidentiality and support family members and other residents during and after
the end-of-life process.
5. Advance Care Planning
- Encourage residents (and families, where appropriate) to discuss preferences for end-of-life care
early in the care process.
- Record wishes regarding resuscitation (DNAR), place of death, spiritual or cultural practices, and
pain management preferences.
- Review plans regularly or when health conditions change.
6. Palliative and Comfort Care
- Prioritise pain and symptom management in accordance with clinical guidelines.
- Offer support from multidisciplinary teams, including palliative care specialists, as needed.
- Facilitate spiritual or religious rites in accordance with the resident's beliefs.
7. Family Involvement
- Include families in care planning where appropriate.
- Provide emotional support and practical information throughout the dying process.
- Allow flexible visiting and overnight stays when a resident is nearing death.
8. Environment and Dignity
- Ensure a peaceful, private, and respectful environment.
- Support cultural, religious, and personal rituals before and after death.
- Handle the deceased with dignity and ensure families are given time to say goodbye.
9. After-Death Protocol
- Notify relevant authorities and professionals promptly.
- Respect the resident's and family's wishes regarding funeral arrangements.
- Offer bereavement support to families and staff.
- Record and report death in accordance with HIQA and legal requirements.
10. Staff Training
All staff will receive training in end-of-life care, including communication skills, symptom
management, and emotional support.
11. Monitoring and Review
- Audits will be conducted annually or following any significant incident.
- Feedback from families and staff will be reviewed to improve care standards.
- This policy will be reviewed annually or sooner if required by changes in legislation or best
practice.
