=====Silverwings_Statement_of_Purpose.docx=====
Statement of Purpose – Silverwings Children’s Residential Centre
1. Name of the Service:
Silverwings Children’s Residential Centre
Sillogue, Monasterboice, Co. Louth
2. Provider:
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
(Private provider operating under Tusla registration for children’s residential care)
3. Person in Charge:
A designated Person in Charge (PIC) holds relevant qualifications and experience in social care management, and is responsible for the day-to-day governance, staffing, quality, and safeguarding across the centre.
4. Purpose and Function of the Centre:
Silverwings Children’s Residential Centre is a privately run, purpose-led service providing safe, nurturing, and therapeutically-informed care to six children and young people aged 6 to 18 years. The centre caters to children who are placed in care under the Child Care Act 1991 (as amended) and may present with complex needs due to trauma, abuse, neglect, or family breakdown.

The overarching aim is to create a homely, structured, and responsive environment where each child can develop socially, emotionally, and educationally through individualised care plans and multi-disciplinary input. We strive to support children to build trust, resilience, and independence while maintaining links with their families, communities, and cultural backgrounds.
5. Staffing and Governance Structure:
To ensure high standards of care and supervision, the staffing structure at Silverwings consists of:

- 1 Person in Charge (PIC) – overseeing governance, compliance, and leadership
- 4 Social Care Workers (SCWs) – supporting care planning, emotional regulation, life skills, and advocacy
- 2 Staff Nurses (RPN/RGN) – overseeing children’s physical and mental health needs in collaboration with external clinicians
- 10 Direct Support Workers (DSWs) – providing 24/7 supervision, daily living support, and safe crisis intervention

Clinical governance support will be provided externally by a consulting psychologist or clinical team as needed.

All staff receive regular supervision, mandatory training, and therapeutic input to maintain reflective practice and uphold HIQA care standards.
6. Admissions and Referral Criteria:
Silverwings accepts referrals via Tusla Placement Teams for children aged 6–18 who require medium- to long-term residential care. The centre is not equipped for high-risk secure placements but can accommodate complex behavioural, emotional, or attachment-based needs with the appropriate care plan in place.

Each placement is preceded by a comprehensive impact risk assessment to ensure the safety and suitability for both the referred child and current residents.
7. Ethos and Values:
Our care is underpinned by trauma-informed practice, child-centred planning, and the belief that every child deserves safety, stability, and a sense of belonging. We value each child’s right to be heard, protected, and supported in line with the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Silverwings works in close collaboration with families, guardians, education providers, and Tusla social work departments to ensure holistic development, education continuity, and positive life outcomes for all young people in our care.

 Statement of Purpose
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Silverwings Emergency & Short-Term Accommodation
Monasterboice, Co. Louth
Tusla-Registered Provision
1. Service Overview
Silverwings, operated by Secure Living Support Services Ltd, offers high-quality emergency and short-term residential accommodation for children and young people under the care of Tusla – the Child and Family Agency. Located in a discreet and secure rural setting in Monasterboice, Co. Louth, Silverwings is designed to provide a safe, calming, and developmentally appropriate environment for vulnerable service users.
2. Aims and Objectives
- To provide safe and immediate accommodation to children in crisis situations.
- To offer a stabilising, nurturing environment with consistent adult support.
- To promote the emotional, physical, and educational wellbeing of each child through structured daily routines and trauma-informed care.
- To work in close collaboration with Tusla, families, and key professionals to support positive transitions to longer-term placements or reunification.
- To uphold the highest standards of care as outlined in the Child Care (Placement of Children in Residential Care) Regulations 1995 and relevant national standards.
3. Target Group
Silverwings is approved to accommodate:
- Children and young people aged 8 to 17 years
- Both male and female service users
- Referrals under emergency, short-term, or medium-term circumstances
- Individuals requiring support due to neglect, family breakdown, homelessness, or behavioural needs not requiring high-secure intervention
4. Model of Care
Silverwings operates under a trauma-informed, relationship-based care model, including:
- 24/7 skilled keyworker support
- Positive Behavioural Supports (PBS)
- Daily life skills training and emotional regulation support
- Education access facilitation
- Support from external professionals (GP, CAMHS, school liaison)
- Culturally sensitive and rights-based care planning
5. The Premises
Silverwings is a former guesthouse converted into a secure care facility comprising:
- 9 private bedrooms, 6 of which are part of two self-contained 3-bed residential units for HIQA registration
- 3 high-need emergency placements in single-occupancy rooms
- Shared living and recreation spaces
- On-site kitchen, utility/laundry rooms, and office space
- External play area and gardens
- Fire safety and health & safety systems compliant with statutory regulations
- Accessible location with proximity to Drogheda, M1, and key community services
6. Staffing & Qualifications
Silverwings is staffed by a dedicated multidisciplinary team, including:
- Registered Service Manager (Social Care Level 8, 5+ years’ experience)
- Social Care Workers (Level 7 minimum)
- Night and Relief Staff
- External Consultant Psychologist (on-call basis)
All staff are:
- Garda vetted and Children First–certified
- Trained in CPI, MAPA, and First Aid
- Supervised under a structured performance management system
7. Governance & Oversight
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is governed by a Director of Services, supported by a Clinical and Quality Assurance team. We ensure:
- Full compliance with Tusla and HIQA standards
- Incident reporting and risk management
- Ongoing internal audits and reviews
- Regular multidisciplinary team (MDT) reviews of care plans and progress
8. Quality and Continuous Improvement
We maintain a continuous improvement framework through:
- Regular policy and procedure updates
- Service user feedback and exit interviews
- Staff training and supervision cycles
- Active participation in Tusla’s quality and performance meetings
- Complaint handling, safeguarding audits, and GDPR compliance
9. Capacity & Registration Plans
- Total rooms: 9
- Registered HIQA beds: 6 (two 3-bedroom units)
- Emergency placement rooms: 3 (single occupancy, high supervision)
- Each resident will have a dedicated care plan, keyworker, and support team.
- Registration is underway with both HIQA (for residential units) and Tusla (for emergency placements).

Statement of Purpose
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Silverwings Emergency & Short-Term Accommodation
Monasterboice, Co. Louth
Tusla-Registered Provision
1. Service Overview
Silverwings, operated by Secure Living Support Services Ltd, offers high-quality emergency and
short-term residential accommodation for children and young people under the care of Tusla - the
Child and Family Agency. Located in a discreet and secure rural setting in Monasterboice, Co.
Louth, Silverwings is designed to provide a safe, calming, and developmentally appropriate
environment for vulnerable service users.
2. Aims and Objectives
- To provide safe and immediate accommodation to children in crisis situations.
- To offer a stabilising, nurturing environment with consistent adult support.
- To promote the emotional, physical, and educational wellbeing of each child through structured
daily routines and trauma-informed care.
- To work in close collaboration with Tusla, families, and key professionals to support positive
transitions to longer-term placements or reunification.
- To uphold the highest standards of care as outlined in the Child Care (Placement of Children in
Residential Care) Regulations 1995 and relevant national standards.
3. Target Group
Silverwings is approved to accommodate:
- Children and young people aged 8 to 17 years
- Both male and female service users
- Referrals under emergency, short-term, or medium-term circumstances
- Individuals requiring support due to neglect, family breakdown, homelessness, or behavioural
needs not requiring high-secure intervention
4. Model of Care
Silverwings operates under a trauma-informed, relationship-based care model, including:
- 24/7 skilled keyworker support
- Positive Behavioural Supports (PBS)
- Daily life skills training and emotional regulation support
- Education access facilitation
- Support from external professionals (GP, CAMHS, school liaison)
- Culturally sensitive and rights-based care planning
5. The Premises
Silverwings is a former guesthouse converted into a secure care facility comprising:
- 9 private bedrooms, 6 of which are part of two self-contained 3-bed residential units for HIQA
registration
- 3 high-need emergency placements in single-occupancy rooms
- Shared living and recreation spaces
- On-site kitchen, utility/laundry rooms, and office space
- External play area and gardens
- Fire safety and health & safety systems compliant with statutory regulations
- Accessible location with proximity to Drogheda, M1, and key community services
6. Staffing & Qualifications
Silverwings is staffed by a dedicated multidisciplinary team, including:
- Registered Service Manager (Social Care Level 8, 5+ years' experience)
- Social Care Workers (Level 7 minimum)
- Night and Relief Staff
- External Consultant Psychologist (on-call basis)
All staff are:
- Garda vetted and Children First-certified
- Trained in CPI, MAPA, and First Aid
- Supervised under a structured performance management system
7. Governance & Oversight
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is governed by a Director of Services, supported by a Clinical
and Quality Assurance team. We ensure:
- Full compliance with Tusla and HIQA standards
- Incident reporting and risk management
- Ongoing internal audits and reviews
- Regular multidisciplinary team (MDT) reviews of care plans and progress
8. Quality and Continuous Improvement
We maintain a continuous improvement framework through:
- Regular policy and procedure updates
- Service user feedback and exit interviews
- Staff training and supervision cycles
- Active participation in Tusla's quality and performance meetings
- Complaint handling, safeguarding audits, and GDPR compliance
9. Capacity & Registration Plans
- Total rooms: 9
- Registered HIQA beds: 6 (two 3-bedroom units)
- Emergency placement rooms: 3 (single occupancy, high supervision)
- Each resident will have a dedicated care plan, keyworker, and support team.
- Registration is underway with both HIQA (for residential units) and Tusla (for emergency
placements).
