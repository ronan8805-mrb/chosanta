Statement of Purpose

Silverwings Children's Residential Centre — Tusla Registered

1 Service Overview

Silverwings is a privately run, purpose-led residential centre providing safe, nurturing, and therapeutically-informed care to children and young people aged 6–18, placed under the Child Care Act 1991. Located in Monasterboice, Co. Louth, operated by Chosanta Management.

2 Aims & Objectives
  • Provide safe, immediate accommodation to children in crisis situations
  • Offer a stabilising, nurturing environment with consistent adult support
  • Promote emotional, physical, and educational wellbeing through structured routines and trauma-informed care
  • Collaborate with Tusla, families, and professionals for positive transitions or reunification
  • Uphold Child Care (Placement of Children in Residential Care) Regulations 1995
3 Target Group & Admissions
  • Children aged 8–17; male and female; emergency, short-term, or medium-term
  • Referrals via Tusla Placement Teams; impact risk assessment precedes every placement
  • Not equipped for high-risk secure placements; accommodates complex behavioural/emotional/attachment needs
4 Model of Care
  • Trauma-informed, relationship-based care model
  • 24/7 skilled keyworker support; Positive Behavioural Supports (PBS)
  • Daily life skills, emotional regulation; education access facilitation
  • External professionals: GP, CAMHS, school liaison; culturally sensitive and rights-based planning
5 Ethos & Values

Underpinned by trauma-informed practice, child-centred planning, and the belief that every child deserves safety, stability, and belonging. We value each child's right to be heard, protected, and supported per the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Staffing, Premises & Capacity

6 Staffing & Governance
  • 1 PIC — governance, compliance, leadership (Social Care Level 8, 5+ years)
  • 4 Social Care Workers — care planning, emotional regulation, life skills, advocacy (Level 7 min)
  • 2 Staff Nurses (RPN/RGN) — physical/mental health, medication, clinical liaison
  • 10 Direct Support Workers — 24/7 supervision, daily living, crisis intervention
  • External Consultant Psychologist on-call; all staff Garda vetted, Children First certified, CPI/MAPA/First Aid trained
7 The Premises

9-bedroom detached property, former guesthouse converted to secure care facility. Fully enclosed with perimeter fencing and gated access.

  • Ground: Reception, living room, kitchen/dining, staff office, 2 bedrooms, bathroom, laundry
  • First Floor: 4 bedrooms, bathroom, staff sleepover with ensuite, therapy/quiet room
  • Second Floor: 3 bedrooms (inc. therapeutic/spare), storage
  • External: Enclosed garden with play area, private driveway (6 vehicles), CCTV (external only, GDPR compliant)
  • Security: Coded access, fire panel, emergency lighting, PAT tested, fire extinguishers marked

Emotional, Spiritual & Quality Improvement

9 Emotional Needs Policy
  • Trauma-informed: Safety, trust, consistency, emotional regulation prioritised
  • Keyworking: Dedicated keyworker; weekly 1:1 sessions; daily emotional check-ins
  • Environment: Predictable, warm home; quiet spaces and sensory resources; TCI/MAPA trained staff
  • Activities: Journaling, music, art, life story work, emotional literacy tools
  • Behaviour as communication: PBS plans; root cause exploration; healthy coping strategies
  • Mental health: CAMHS referrals, counsellors, play therapists; strategies in Care Plan
  • Voice: Weekly house meetings, suggestion box, advocacy links, child-centred planning
10 Spiritual & Cultural Needs
  • Spiritual/religious affiliation assessed on admission via dedicated form
  • Attendance at services/festivals facilitated; prayer mats, texts, private space provided
  • Transport to religious/cultural events; access to faith leaders when requested
  • Staff trained in cultural competence; respectful, non-judgmental dialogue
