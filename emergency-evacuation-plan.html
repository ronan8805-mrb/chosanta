Emergency Evacuation Plan

Sections 1–5: Purpose, Scope, Emergencies, Alarm & Procedure

1 Purpose

To ensure the safe and efficient evacuation of all occupants in the event of an emergency, minimising risk to life and enabling rapid response by emergency services.

2 Scope

Applies to all staff, residents/service users, contractors, and visitors present in the premises at any time.

3 Emergency Types Covered
  • Fire or Smoke
  • Gas Leak
  • Bomb Threat
  • Medical Emergency
  • Severe Weather / Flood
  • Security Threat or Intruder
4 Alarm System
  • Type: Fire alarm / manual call point / smoke detection
  • Audible & visual alarms located throughout the premises
  • All staff trained to recognise and respond immediately
5 Evacuation Procedure
  • Step 1 — Raise the Alarm: Activate the nearest fire alarm point or call out loudly. Call 999/112 and state the emergency type, location, and any hazards.
  • Step 2 — Immediate Action: Stop all activities, leave belongings behind, assist residents to evacuate — prioritising those with mobility or sensory impairments.
  • Step 3 — Use of Exits: Follow illuminated exit signs to the nearest safe exit. Do not use lifts. Close doors behind you to slow the spread of fire/smoke.
  • Step 4 — Assembly Point: Proceed to the designated assembly point. Stay in a group and await instructions.
  • Step 5 — Roll Call: Person in Charge or Fire Warden to take register. Report anyone missing to emergency services immediately.

Emergency Evacuation Plan

Sections 6–11: Responsibilities, Accessibility, Training & Review

6 Responsibilities
  • Person in Charge / Fire Warden: Lead evacuation and ensure building is cleared; take roll call; liaise with emergency services
  • Staff: Follow evacuation routes; assist service users; do not re-enter the building until declared safe
  • Visitors: Must be escorted to the assembly point by staff
7 Assistance for Persons with Disabilities
  • Assign staff "buddies" to assist individuals with mobility or sensory needs
  • Ensure evacuation chairs or mobility aids are positioned at stairwells
8 Training & Drills
  • Evacuation drills every 6 months
  • New staff trained during induction
  • Records maintained
9 Plan Display
  • Evacuation routes and assembly points displayed in all rooms and corridors
  • Laminated copies in reception and staff areas
10 Contact Information
  • Emergency Services: 999 / 112
  • On-Site Emergency Coordinator: [Name & Phone]
  • Backup Coordinator: [Name & Phone]
11 Review

Reviewed annually or after major incidents or layout changes.
