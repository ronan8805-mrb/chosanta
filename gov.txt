Governance Policy
1. Purpose
The purpose of this Governance Policy is to define the governance framework, roles, and
responsibilities of Secure Living Support Services Ltd. This policy ensures that the organisation
operates in line with the Health Act 2007, the National Standards for Residential Services for
Children and Adults with Disabilities, and all other applicable regulatory frameworks.
Governance is the system by which the service is directed, controlled, and held accountable. It
ensures that the organisation delivers safe, effective, and person-centred care to all individuals
supported.
Specifically, this policy is designed to:
- Ensure a clear line of accountability across all levels of leadership and service delivery.
- Promote transparent decision-making based on legal, ethical, and best-practice frameworks.
- Establish systems for monitoring performance, managing risk, and safeguarding people who use
the service.
- Support continuous quality improvement through leadership, planning, oversight, and
communication.
- Ensure that roles such as the Registered Provider, Person in Charge (PIC), and Governance
Officer are fully defined, resourced, and understood.
This policy underpins all other operational procedures and policies and serves as the foundation for
maintaining high standards of care and compliance across the organisation.

- Person in Charge (PIC)

- Designated Centre(s)

- Immediately following any material organisational change.
- In response to recommendations from HIQA or internal audits.
This governance model ensures Secure Living Support Services Ltd meets all legislative and ethical

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Governance Policy - Section 3: Responsibilities
This section outlines the defined roles and responsibilities of key personnel within Secure Living
Support Services Ltd. These responsibilities are structured to ensure that all legal, operational, and
safeguarding obligations are fulfilled.
1. Registered Provider
- Has overall accountability for the operation of the designated centre.
- Ensures compliance with the Health Act 2007 and all relevant regulations and standards.
- Oversees the appointment and performance of the Person in Charge (PIC).
- Provides appropriate resources (staffing, finance, premises) to meet residents' needs.
- Submits required notifications and reports to HIQA and other authorities.
2. Director (Daisy Cannon)
- Provides strategic direction and oversight of governance, finance, and service delivery.
- Conducts annual governance reviews and ensures quality improvement planning.
- Monitors compliance with policies and risk management systems.
- Ensures that audit findings, complaints, and incidents inform future planning.
3. Operational Governance Officer (Ian Devine)
- Oversees internal audits, policy compliance, and continuous improvement processes.
- Supports the PIC in implementing best practices across the service.
- Acts as lead liaison with HIQA inspectors and regulatory bodies.
- Provides escalation and support for any safeguarding, staffing, or risk issues.
4. Person in Charge (PIC)
- Manages the day-to-day operations of the designated centre.
- Ensures care is delivered in line with personal plans and national standards.
- Supervises and supports all staff to maintain safe and person-centred care.
- Maintains up-to-date records, submits notifications, and addresses resident concerns.
- Implements safeguarding policies and promotes resident rights and wellbeing.
5. Multidisciplinary and Support Staff
- Work collaboratively under the PIC to deliver direct care and support.
- Participate in training, supervision, and team meetings.
- Adhere to policies, procedures, and the values of Secure Living Support Services Ltd.
- Support residents to achieve goals and promote independence.
This structure ensures that all staff understand their responsibilities and that governance functions
operate transparently, effectively, and in the best interests of the people we support.

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Governance Policy - Section 5: Governance Review
5. Governance Review
Secure Living Support Services Ltd conducts ongoing and structured governance reviews to ensure
the effectiveness of leadership, compliance with regulatory requirements, and continuous
improvement across all areas of service provision.
5.1 Purpose of the Governance Review
- Evaluate the effectiveness of governance systems and leadership structures.
- Identify risks, areas for improvement, and successes in service delivery.
- Ensure compliance with Regulation 23 (Governance & Management) of the Health Act 2007.
- Update the organisation's Strategic and Quality Improvement Plans based on findings.
5.2 Review Frequency
- Formal Governance Reviews are held quarterly.
- A comprehensive annual review is conducted each calendar year.
- Extraordinary reviews may be held following serious incidents or HIQA inspections.
5.3 Scope of Review
- Legal and regulatory compliance.
- Leadership structure and oversight effectiveness.
- Audit findings and follow-up actions.
- Quality of resident care.
- Staff supervision and development.
- Feedback from service users, families, and staff.
- Incident and complaint management.
- Health and safety.
- Budgeting and resource use.
5.4 Review Process
- Governance Officer prepares internal audit reports.
- Data from logs, complaints, and feedback are reviewed.
- An action plan is created and shared with stakeholders.
- Outcomes are recorded and tracked.
5.5 Record-Keeping
- Reviews are minuted and signed by the Chair and Governance Officer.
- Records are securely stored and accessible to HIQA.
- Actions are logged in the Governance Action Register.
5.6 Outcomes
- Drives quality improvements.
- Updates policies and procedures.
- Supports risk mitigation planning.
- Enhances staff training and support.
- Provides inspection-ready governance evidence.
