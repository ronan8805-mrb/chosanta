Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Intimate Care Policy
Section 1: Purpose
1. Purpose
The purpose of this Intimate Care Policy is to ensure that all intimate care provided to residents of
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is delivered in a manner that protects dignity, privacy,
independence, and wellbeing.
This policy outlines the standards and procedures for supporting residents with tasks of a personal
nature including, but not limited to:
- Personal hygiene
- Continence care
- Dressing and undressing
- Menstrual care
- Support with eating or drinking if required
The policy aims to promote a respectful, safe, and person-centred approach in accordance with
HIQA standards and safeguarding requirements.

Section 2: Scope
2. Scope
This policy applies to all staff at Secure Living Support Services Ltd who are involved in providing or
supporting residents with any form of intimate care. It also provides guidance for managers, trainers,
and any professionals involved in overseeing personal support plans.
It covers:
- Residents of all genders and ages with personal care needs
- One-to-one and assisted care delivery
- The use of continence aids and bathing equipment
- Respecting gender, cultural, and religious sensitivities
- Situations involving consent, risk, and protection from abuse
All staff are expected to follow this policy and related procedures to uphold resident dignity and
safety.

Section 3: Principles of Intimate Care
3. Principles of Intimate Care
The following principles guide the delivery of intimate care in Secure Living Support Services Ltd:
- Dignity and Respect: Every resident has the right to privacy and dignity at all times.
- Consent and Choice: Intimate care is only provided with resident consent, and they are supported
to express preferences.
- Independence: Residents are encouraged and supported to carry out aspects of care themselves
where possible.
- Safety: Practices follow infection control standards and are risk-assessed to ensure health and
safety.
- Communication: Staff explain procedures and ensure residents are comfortable and informed.
- Gender Sensitivity: Resident requests regarding the gender of the carer are respected where
feasible.
- Professionalism: Staff conduct themselves with discretion, empathy, and awareness of personal
boundaries.
All intimate care is documented appropriately and provided with the utmost sensitivity and integrity.

Section 4: Consent
4. Consent
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises the right of every resident to give or withhold consent
for intimate care. Obtaining valid consent is a core element of safe and respectful practice.
Key practices include:
- Seeking verbal or non-verbal consent before each instance of intimate care
- Respecting a resident's right to refuse or withdraw consent at any time
- Involving residents in decision-making and acknowledging their preferences
- Supporting individuals to communicate consent in their preferred method (e.g. gestures, signs,
assistive devices)
- Where capacity is in question, a formal capacity assessment must be carried out and best interest
decisions documented in line with the Assisted Decision-Making (Capacity) Act 2015
- Consulting with families, representatives, or advocates only where consent and legal permissions
allow
Consent is documented clearly in personal care plans and reviewed regularly.

Section 5: Staff Practice
5. Staff Practice
All staff at Secure Living Support Services Ltd are expected to follow professional, person-centred,
and safe procedures when delivering intimate care.
Best practice standards include:
- Explaining each step of the process and checking for consent before proceeding
- Ensuring privacy by closing doors, using screens, and limiting exposure
- Using gloves, aprons, and appropriate PPE to maintain hygiene
- Using soft tone, calm manner, and respectful language
- Encouraging independence by allowing residents to perform parts of the task
- Never rushing or pressuring a resident to accept support
- Reporting any unusual findings or concerns (e.g., bruising, skin changes) immediately
- Documenting care provided clearly and factually
Staff must maintain professional boundaries and avoid behaviour that could be misunderstood or
cause distress.

Section 6: Privacy & Dignity
6. Privacy & Dignity
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to protecting the privacy and dignity of residents at
all times during the provision of intimate care.
Key practices include:
- Providing care in private spaces with minimal interruptions
- Knocking before entering and explaining intentions clearly
- Covering the resident appropriately and exposing only what is necessary for care
- Using calm, reassuring, and respectful communication throughout
- Allowing residents time to respond, participate, or ask questions
- Encouraging independence to the extent possible while preserving self-esteem
- Accommodating cultural and personal preferences regarding gender and routines
Every resident has the right to feel safe, valued, and respected during all aspects of care. These
standards are reinforced through training, observation, and supervision.

Section 7: Record Keeping
7. Record Keeping
Accurate and timely documentation of intimate care is essential for accountability, communication,
and resident safety.
Staff must:
- Record each instance of intimate care provided in the daily care notes or relevant documentation
system
- Include the time, nature of the care, and any observations (e.g. skin integrity, toileting needs,
reactions)
- Report any concerns, injuries, refusals, or unusual findings to the Person in Charge immediately
- Use objective, factual, and respectful language in all entries
- Maintain confidentiality at all times
Records must be stored securely and be accessible only to authorised personnel in line with GDPR
and HIQA standards.

Section 8: Safeguarding & Reporting Concerns
8. Safeguarding & Reporting Concerns
Secure Living Support Services Ltd prioritises the safety and protection of all residents during the
delivery of intimate care. Staff must be vigilant for any signs of distress, discomfort, or potential
abuse.
Key procedures include:
- Reporting any concerns, disclosures, or suspicions immediately to the Designated Liaison Person
(DLP) or Person in Charge
- Documenting concerns factually and confidentially
- Following the organisation's safeguarding policy and Children First/National Safeguarding
guidelines
- Protecting the resident from further harm while the concern is being addressed
- Cooperating fully with any internal or external investigations
- Ensuring the resident has access to advocacy and support where needed
All safeguarding reports are treated seriously, investigated promptly, and followed through in
accordance with legal obligations.

Section 9: Training
9. Training
Secure Living Support Services Ltd ensures that all staff involved in providing intimate care receive
appropriate training to carry out their duties safely, respectfully, and competently.
Mandatory training includes:
- Intimate care procedures and personal care planning
- Infection prevention and control
- Safeguarding vulnerable adults and children
- Consent, dignity, and communication in care
- Recognising and responding to distress, refusal, or changes in behaviour
Refresher training is provided annually or as required based on observed practice or changes in
guidance. Staff must demonstrate understanding and apply principles in daily practice.

Section 10: Policy Review
10. Policy Review
This Intimate Care Policy is reviewed annually or sooner if there are changes in legislation, HIQA
standards, safeguarding guidance, or organisational practice.
The review process involves:
- Feedback from residents, staff, and advocates
- Review of incident reports and practice audits
- Incorporation of learning from training, inspections, or complaints
- Oversight by the Registered Provider and Person in Charge
All revised versions are communicated to staff and made available to residents in appropriate
formats.
