Medication Audit

Monthly Internal Audit — S.I. 674/2017 · HIQA National Standards 2018

Audit DateAuditorRole
Period CoveredCentreNo. of Residents

This audit maps to the Chosanta Medication Management Policy and the Child Care (Standards in Children's Residential Centres) Regulations 2018. Each area is scored: C = Compliant · SC = Substantially Compliant · NC = Non-Compliant · N/A = Not Applicable

1 Prescribing & Administration Records
CriteriaC/SC/NC/NAEvidence / Comment
Prescription sheets accurate, current, and signed by authorised prescriber
Medication Administration Records (MARs) completed for every dose
PRN (as-needed) medications have clear instructions and frequency limits
Dose administered matches prescription — no unauthorised variations
Medication given at prescribed times; late/missed doses documented
Staff administering medication have completed Medication Management training
2 Storage & Security
CriteriaC/SC/NC/NAEvidence / Comment
Medications stored in locked cabinet at correct temperature
Controlled drugs stored in double-locked cabinet; keys held by named person
Medications labelled clearly with child's name, dose, and instructions
Expired medications identified and disposed of correctly
Medication stock reconciled — counts match records
3 Incidents, Errors & Self-Administration
CriteriaC/SC/NC/NAEvidence / Comment
Medication errors / near misses reported and recorded via NIMS/SEN
Root cause analysis completed for any medication errors since last audit
Children self-administering have a current risk assessment on file
Parent/guardian consent obtained for all medications administered

Fire Drill & Safety Log

Quarterly Fire Drill Record — Fire Services Act 1981/2003 · HIQA Standard 3

HIQA inspectors examine fire drill records and Personal Emergency Evacuation Plans (PEEPs) as a core safety evidence requirement. Drills must include all staff and residents and must be conducted at varied times (day/night). PEEPs must be reviewed bi-annually and on each new admission.
