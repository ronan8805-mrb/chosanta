Medication Management Policy

Sections 1–3: Purpose, Scope & Responsibilities

1 Purpose

To ensure the safe, effective, and accountable management of medications within Chosanta Management, covering prescribing, storage, administration, and disposal in accordance with legislative and best practice guidelines.

2 Scope
  • Registered Nurses, HCAs, PICs, GPs, pharmacists, agency staff, and management
  • Prescribing, ordering, receiving, storing, administering, documenting, auditing, and disposing of medications
  • All prescribed, non-prescribed, controlled, and PRN (as-needed) medications
3 Responsibilities
  • Registered Provider: Oversees policy; ensures governance systems, resources, and training
  • PIC: Ensures correct protocols; reviews records/audits; reports errors; coordinates with pharmacy/GP
  • Registered Nurses: Administer safely per NMBI guidelines; record immediately and accurately; report errors
  • HCAs: Assist with self-administration if trained; monitor for side effects; do not administer unless delegated
  • GPs & Pharmacists: Accurate legible prescriptions; medication reviews; compliance support
  • All Staff: Report errors/risks; attend mandatory training; work within scope

Section 4: Prescribing & Ordering

4 Prescribing & Ordering

4.1 Prescribing Standards:

  • Only qualified registered medical practitioners may prescribe medication
  • Prescriptions must be clear, legible, signed — include resident name, date, drug, dose, route, frequency, duration
  • Verbal/telephone prescriptions not accepted except emergencies — must be documented and signed within 24 hours

4.2 Medication Reviews:

  • Every 3–6 months, or sooner if clinically indicated or following significant health changes
  • Conducted by GP and/or pharmacist in collaboration with nursing staff

4.3 Ordering Procedures:

  • Ordered through approved community pharmacy with formal agreement
  • Responsibility of Registered Nurse or PIC
  • All orders checked against current prescription, recorded in Medication Order Log, signed and dated

Section 5: Administration

5 Administration of Medication

Five Rights of Administration:

  • Right Resident · Right Medication · Right Dose · Right Time · Right Route

5.2 Administration Process:

  • MAR checked before and after each administration
  • Medication administered directly to resident — never left unattended
  • Documentation completed immediately after giving medication, not before

5.3 PRN (As Needed) Medications:

  • Clearly prescribed with maximum dose and frequency
  • Reason for administration and outcome documented in notes and MAR

5.4 Covert Administration:

  • Only permitted after full MDT assessment, documented covert care plan, and where resident lacks capacity
  • Must be authorised in writing and reviewed regularly

5.5 Refusal of Medication:

  • Respect resident's choice unless they lack capacity
  • Document refusal in MAR; inform GP and PIC if repeated or serious

Sections 6–7: Storage & Documentation

6 Storage of Medication
  • General: Locked cabinet/trolley in designated room with limited access; correct temperatures per manufacturer
  • Refrigerated: Dedicated medication fridge; temperature checked daily and recorded; original packaging
  • Controlled Drugs: Double-locked CD cabinet per Misuse of Drugs Regulations; CD register completed for every administration, signed by two staff, audited weekly
  • Expired/Discontinued: Clearly marked, separated from in-use stock, returned to pharmacist and logged
7 Recording & Documentation
  • MAR Sheet: Maintained for each resident — time, dose, route, initials, omission/refusal reasons, PRN outcomes; updated in real time, reviewed weekly
  • Medication Error Log: Type of error, date/time, actions taken, staff involved, whether GP/family informed
  • CD Register: Entries in ink immediately after administration; signed by two staff; balanced end of each shift
  • Retention: All medication records retained minimum 7 years, available for HIQA inspection

Sections 8–11: Errors, Disposal, Review & Training

8 Medication Errors
  • Ensure resident safety first; contact GP/emergency services if needed; inform PIC immediately
  • Document in Error Log and resident notes; all errors reported regardless of harm
  • PIC investigates, initiates corrective actions, informs GP/family/regulators as required
  • Errors reviewed at governance meetings; may trigger policy updates, training, or enhanced supervision
9 Disposal of Medication
  • Remove expired, discontinued, damaged, or unidentifiable medications from active use
  • Store awaiting disposal in locked, labelled container separate from in-use stock
  • Return to nominated pharmacist; complete disposal log (name, medication, quantity, reason, signatures)
  • Controlled Drugs: Two staff signatures; witnessed destruction by pharmacist; separate CD disposal record
10 Review & Audit
  • Policy reviewed annually or following new guidance, incidents, or audit gaps
  • Monthly medication audits: MAR completion, storage, PRN/CD logs, disposal logs, error documentation
  • PIC conducts periodic spot checks during medication rounds
11 Staff Training & Competency
  • Mandatory HIQA-compliant training before administering any medication; refreshed every 2 years
  • Practical competency assessment: administration, MAR charts, CD handling, storage, ordering, disposal
  • PIC signs off each staff member's competency; training logs maintained by management
