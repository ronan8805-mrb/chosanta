Social, Recreational & Community Participation

Comprehensive Policy — HIQA Compliant

1 Purpose

To promote and support the right of all residents to participate in meaningful social, recreational, and community-based activities that enhance quality of life, foster inclusion, promote wellbeing, and support independence and personal choice.

2 Scope
  • All service users, staff, management, and volunteers
  • Group and individual recreational programmes; education, volunteering, employment access
  • Community engagement, cultural events, outings, holidays, special occasions
  • Partnerships with local organisations, clubs, and social services
3 Principles
  • Person-Centred: Based on individual interests, abilities, preferences, and support needs
  • Inclusion: Supported to engage with wider community and form meaningful relationships
  • Empowerment: Encouraged to set personal goals and participate in activity planning
  • Choice: Right to decline participation without judgement or pressure
  • Diversity & Culture: Activities reflect and respect cultural, religious, and personal backgrounds
  • Safety: Risk-assessed with trained staff; Equality: Equal opportunities regardless of ability
4 Assessment & Planning
  • Social/recreational needs assessed as part of holistic personal support plan
  • Resident involvement in identifying goals and interests; keyworker input into scheduling
  • Collaboration with families, advocates, and professionals; documented in personal plan/activity log
  • Ongoing review and adjustment based on resident feedback and outcomes
5 Examples of Activities
  • Arts & crafts, group games, music workshops, walks, picnics, nature outings
  • Community events, theatre, cinema, social clubs, sports, drama groups
  • Volunteering, part-time supported employment, life skills (shopping, budgeting, cooking)
  • Cultural, religious, or seasonal celebrations; visits to library, café, gym

Support, Resources, Rights & Review

6 Support & Supervision
  • Supervision needs assessed within individual plan; named staff assigned for off-site/group activities
  • Clear instructions, guidance, reassurance; sufficient staff ratios for safety and inclusion
  • Gradual independence-building; dignity and privacy balanced with health & safety oversight
7 Resources & Transport
  • Budget allocation for materials, event entry, and activity expenses
  • Accessible vehicles maintained to H&S standards; insurance and risk assessments in place
  • Transport scheduled in advance; mobility/sensory support during travel
  • Partnerships with community transport services explored; resources reviewed regularly
8 Inclusion & Rights
  • Right to choose how to spend time; access local community services and facilities
  • Right to build/maintain personal relationships; privacy, dignity, and autonomy during social experiences
  • Staff actively address barriers (physical, attitudinal, financial) and advocate for full participation
9 Monitoring
  • Activity plans reviewed during care plan reviews; participation documented in daily logs
  • Resident/family feedback via meetings, surveys, keyworker check-ins
  • Audits of activity schedules; analysis of participation trends and barriers to engagement
10 Policy Review
  • Reviewed annually or earlier if prompted by legislation, standards, or audits
  • Consultation with residents, staff, management; review of participation data and incidents
  • Revisions approved by senior management; superseded versions archived per document control
