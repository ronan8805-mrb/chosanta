Sanctions & Consequences Log

HIQA Standard 3 · UNCRC Articles 19 & 37 · S.I. 674/2017

All behavioural consequences and sanctions applied to children must be recorded, proportionate, and reviewed. HIQA inspectors closely examine this log for evidence of proportionality, consistency, respect for the child's dignity, and whether sanctions are being used punitively or as part of a therapeutic approach.

⚠ Prohibited Sanctions: The following must NEVER be used as sanctions in any circumstances:
• Corporal punishment of any kind  • Deprivation of food, drink, or sleep  • Restriction of family contact as punishment
• Locking a child in a room  • Use of isolation as punishment  • Humiliation, ridicule, or degrading treatment
• Collective punishment of all children for one child's behaviour  • Withdrawal of medical care or therapeutic support
✓ Therapeutic Approach: Chosanta Management uses a restorative, therapeutic approach to behaviour management. Consequences should be natural/logical, help the child learn, maintain the relationship, and be agreed with the child where possible. The focus is on understanding behaviour (what happened, why, what was the need behind it) rather than punishment.
