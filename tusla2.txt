Tusla Policy Submission – Secure Living Support Services Ltd
1. Service Name & Purpose
Service Name: Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Purpose: To provide safe, trauma-informed residential care and emergency accommodation to children under the care of Tusla, including placements under Section 36 and Section 5.
1. Purpose
To ensure all children in our care are protected from harm, abuse, and neglect. This policy outlines procedures for recognising, responding to, and reporting child protection concerns.
2. Scope
Applies to all staff, volunteers, contractors, and any person working on behalf of the organisation who may come into contact with children.
3. Policy Statement
We are committed to safeguarding all children in our service and believe every child has the right to live free from abuse, exploitation, and neglect.
4. Definitions
Child: A person under the age of 18
Abuse: Can be physical, emotional, sexual, or neglect
Designated Liaison Person (DLP): Staff member appointed to receive and act on child protection concerns
5. Roles and Responsibilities
All Staff:
- Must report concerns immediately to the DLP
- Must complete mandatory child protection training
DLP:
- Maintains records of all concerns
- Liaises with Tusla and An Garda Síochána
- Ensures follow-up and protection measures are in place
6. Recognising Abuse
Signs include:
- Unexplained injuries
- Withdrawn or fearful behaviour
- Age-inappropriate sexual behaviour or knowledge
- Neglect of hygiene, clothing, food, or supervision
7. Reporting Procedure
Step 1: Staff member informs DLP immediately
Step 2: DLP completes Child Protection and Welfare Report Form
Step 3: Report submitted to Tusla via Portal or directly
Step 4: DLP follows up and records outcomes
In emergency: Contact Gardaí directly
8. Confidentiality
All concerns are handled sensitively and confidentially. Information is shared only on a need-to-know basis in accordance with GDPR and Children First guidelines.
9. Recruitment and Vetting
All staff undergo:
- Garda vetting
- Reference checks
- Induction on child protection policies
10. Training
All staff must complete:
- Children First E-Learning Programme
- Annual refresher training on child safeguarding practices

11. Record Keeping
All concerns, disclosures, and actions taken must be documented and stored securely for 7 years, accessible only to DLP and Manager.

13. Complaints Policy (Tusla Format)
We operate a clear and confidential complaints process accessible to children and families.
How to Make a Complaint:
- Speak to any staff member
- Use anonymous complaint box in hallway
- Contact the Designated Complaints Officer
Response Timeline:
- Acknowledged within 3 working days
- Full response within 10 working days
- Escalation to Manager if unresolved
All complaints logged, tracked, and reviewed monthly.
14. Anti-Bullying Policy
All children have the right to feel safe and supported.
Bullying Includes:
- Physical or verbal abuse
- Online/social media threats
- Peer exclusion or intimidation
Procedure:
- Immediate intervention and recording
- Safety plan created
- Restorative sessions if appropriate
- Parent/Tusla informed
Reviewed after each incident.
15. Positive Behaviour Support Policy
Our approach:
- Use of individualised Behaviour Support Plans (BSP)
- De-escalation training for all staff
- Consistent reward and consequence system
- Daily check-ins with child
No physical or punitive sanctions permitted.
16. Missing Child Protocol
Steps:
1. Notify Manager and search local area
2. Contact Gardaí and parent/Tusla within 15 minutes
3. Record all actions and complete Incident Report
4. On return, hold debrief and safety planning with child
All absconsion events reviewed within 24 hours.

17. Staff Code of Conduct
Staff must:
- Maintain child-centred, professional relationships
- Use respectful tone and language
- Never share personal contact info
- Wear visible ID badges
- Immediately report all safeguarding concerns
18. Data Protection & Confidentiality
All records are:
- Stored securely (locked file room and encrypted drives)
- Accessed by authorised staff only
- Retained for 7 years per Tusla guidelines
Consent is obtained for:
- Photography
- Data sharing
- Family contact info
Our policy aligns with GDPR (2018) and Children First Act (2015).
