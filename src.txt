Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Section 1: Purpose
1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to promote and support the right of all residents at Secure Living
Support Services Ltd to participate in meaningful social, recreational, and community-based
activities that enhance their quality of life.
This policy aims to:
- Foster inclusion and social connectedness
- Promote physical, emotional, and psychological wellbeing
- Support independence and personal choice
- Ensure access to age-appropriate, culturally sensitive, and individually tailored activities
- Align with HIQA National Standards for residential services for people with disabilities and older
persons

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Section 2: Scope
2. Scope
This policy applies to all service users, staff, management, and volunteers at Secure Living Support
Services Ltd. It covers the planning, provision, and support of all social, recreational, and community
participation activities across residential services.
It includes:
- Group and individual recreational programmes
- Access to education, volunteering, and employment
- Community engagement and cultural events
- Outings, holidays, and special occasions
- Partnerships with local organisations, clubs, and social services
The policy supports the right of all residents to access opportunities that enhance their inclusion and
sense of purpose.

=====Social_Recreation_Community_Policy_Section3_Principles.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Section 3: Principles
3. Principles
The following principles guide all social, recreational, and community activities within Secure Living
Support Services Ltd:
- Person-Centredness: Activities are based on individual interests, abilities, preferences, and
support needs.
- Inclusion: Residents are supported to engage with the wider community and form meaningful
relationships.
- Empowerment: Residents are encouraged to set personal goals and participate in activity planning.
- Choice: Residents have the right to decline participation without judgement or pressure.
- Diversity & Culture: Activities reflect and respect cultural, religious, and personal backgrounds.
- Safety: Activities are risk-assessed and staff are trained to provide safe, supported participation.
- Equality: All residents are offered equal opportunities to participate regardless of ability or
background.

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Section 4: Assessment & Planning
4. Assessment & Planning
Each resident's social and recreational needs are assessed as part of their holistic personal support
plan. These assessments consider the individual's interests, strengths, abilities, communication
style, support needs, and cultural preferences.
The planning process includes:
- Resident involvement in identifying goals and interests
- Keyworker or support team input into goal setting and scheduling
- Collaboration with families, advocates, and professionals as appropriate
- Clear documentation in the resident's personal plan or activity log
- Ongoing review and adjustment of activities based on resident feedback and outcomes
Planning ensures access to meaningful and fulfilling experiences that support wellbeing and
inclusion.

=====Social_Recreation_Community_Policy_Section6_Support_Supervision.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Section 6: Support & Supervision
6. Support & Supervision
Secure Living Support Services Ltd ensures that all residents participating in social, recreational,
and community activities receive appropriate levels of support and supervision based on their
individual needs and risk assessments.
Key provisions include:
- Assessing supervision needs as part of the resident's individual plan
- Assigning named staff to accompany residents during off-site or group activities
- Providing clear instructions, guidance, and reassurance during activities
- Ensuring staff ratios are sufficient for safety, inclusion, and responsiveness
- Supporting residents to gradually build independence and confidence where possible
- Promoting dignity and privacy while maintaining oversight for health and safety
All staff are trained to balance encouragement and autonomy with safeguarding duties.

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Section 7: Resources & Transport
7. Resources & Transport
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises that access to meaningful activities depends on
adequate resources and safe, accessible transport options.
Key practices include:
- Budget allocation for recreational materials, event entry, and activity-related expenses
- Use of accessible vehicles maintained in accordance with health and safety standards
- Scheduling transport and staff availability in advance for planned outings
- Ensuring insurance and risk assessments are in place for transport use
- Supporting residents with mobility or sensory needs during travel
- Exploring partnerships with community transport services or volunteers
Resources are reviewed regularly to ensure they meet the evolving needs and interests of residents.

=====Social_Recreation_Community_Policy_Section8_Inclusion_Rights.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Section 8: Inclusion & Rights
8. Inclusion & Rights
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to upholding the rights of all residents to access
and participate in community life in accordance with national standards and human rights principles.
Core rights include:
- The right to choose how to spend time and engage in meaningful activities
- The right to access local community services, resources, and facilities
- The right to build and maintain personal relationships
- The right to privacy, dignity, and autonomy during social experiences
- The right to express views and preferences about participation and engagement
Staff actively promote inclusion by addressing barriers (physical, attitudinal, financial) and
advocating for full participation. Residents are supported to be active citizens within their
communities.

=====Social_Recreation_Community_Policy_Section9_Monitoring.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Section 9: Monitoring
9. Monitoring
Secure Living Support Services Ltd monitors social, recreational, and community participation to
ensure all residents are receiving opportunities that meet their interests, needs, and personal goals.
Monitoring methods include:
- Review of individual activity plans during care plan reviews
- Staff documentation of participation and outcomes in daily logs
- Resident and family feedback through meetings, surveys, or keyworker check-ins
- Audits of group and individual activity schedules
- Analysis of participation trends and any barriers to engagement
Findings inform future planning and continuous improvement in activity provision.

=====Social_Recreation_Community_Policy_Section10_Policy_Review.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Section 10: Policy Review
10. Policy Review
This policy will be reviewed annually or earlier if prompted by changes in legislation, national
standards, resident needs, or internal audits.
The review process includes:
- Consultation with residents, staff, and management
- Review of participation data, feedback, and incident reports
- Incorporation of learning from service evaluations or inspections
- Approval of revisions by senior management
Revised versions will be communicated to all staff, and superseded versions archived in accordance
with document control procedures.
