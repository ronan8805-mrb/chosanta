Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Nutrition & Hydration Policy
Section 2: Scope
2. Scope
This policy applies to all staff working within Secure Living Support Services Ltd who are involved in
the planning, preparation, delivery, and monitoring of nutrition and hydration for service users.
The policy covers:
- Meal planning and provision
- Food safety and hygiene
- Monitoring of nutritional intake
- Assessment of hydration and fluid balance
- Management of special dietary needs, including allergies, religious and cultural preferences, and
medically indicated diets
- Staff responsibilities in supporting residents during mealtimes and ensuring dignity, choice, and
nutritional adequacy

=====Nutrition_Hydration_Policy_Section3_Responsibilities.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Nutrition & Hydration Policy
Section 3: Responsibilities
3. Responsibilities
Secure Living Support Services Ltd ensures that clear roles and responsibilities are assigned to
promote safe and effective nutrition and hydration practices across the service.
Key responsibilities include:
- **Registered Provider & Person in Charge (PIC):**
  Ensures adequate resources, training, and monitoring systems are in place to support nutrition and
hydration care.
- **Care Staff:**
  Responsible for supporting residents during meals, observing dietary intake, recording food/fluid
charts, and reporting concerns.
- **Catering Staff (if applicable):**
  Prepares meals according to dietary plans and ensures food safety and hygiene standards are
met.
- **Healthcare Professionals (e.g., Dietitians, GPs):**
  Assess individual needs, provide care plans, and oversee clinical nutrition support as required.
- **All Staff:**
  Promote dignity and choice, respect cultural or religious food preferences, and adhere to infection
control and food hygiene protocols.

Section 4: Meal Provision
4. Meal Provision
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to ensuring that all service users receive regular,
nutritious, and appealing meals that meet their individual dietary needs and preferences.
Meal provision standards include:
- Three main meals per day with optional snacks and drinks available between meals
- A variety of choices that respect cultural, religious, medical, and personal preferences
- Menus planned in consultation with service users, dietitians, or relevant professionals
- Meals that are well-presented, nutritious, and in accordance with national dietary guidelines
- Appropriate texture-modified diets provided as per assessment (e.g. soft or pureed)
- Hydration encouraged through regular access to clean drinking water and other fluids
Mealtimes are treated as social and dignified occasions, with staff support provided respectfully as
needed.

Section 5: Resident Involvement
5. Resident Involvement
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises that meaningful involvement of residents in decisions
about their meals enhances wellbeing, autonomy, and satisfaction.
To support resident involvement:
- Residents are encouraged to participate in menu planning and give feedback on meal quality
- Food preference forms are completed on admission and reviewed regularly
- Special occasions, cultural celebrations, and personal milestones are acknowledged in meal
offerings
- Residents may, where safe and appropriate, assist in meal preparation or table setting to enhance
independence
- Residents are supported to raise concerns or make suggestions about food and drink provision
through regular forums or keyworker discussions
Resident feedback is regularly reviewed by management and catering staff to improve food quality
and dining experience.

Section 6: Special Diets
6. Special Diets
Secure Living Support Services Ltd ensures that residents with special dietary requirements receive
safe, appropriate, and well-balanced meals tailored to their individual health, cultural, or personal
needs.
Special diets may include:
- Medically prescribed diets (e.g., diabetic, low sodium, gluten-free)
- Texture-modified diets (e.g., pureed, minced and moist, soft)
- Culturally or religiously influenced diets (e.g., halal, kosher, vegetarian)
- Allergy or intolerance-based exclusions (e.g., lactose intolerance, nut-free)
Key actions include:
- Clear documentation of dietary needs in care plans
- Staff training on allergy awareness and food handling precautions
- Kitchen staff involvement in understanding and preparing special meals
- Ongoing communication between care teams, dietitians, and residents to ensure needs are met
safely and respectfully

Section 7: Hydration Management
7. Hydration Management
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to maintaining optimal hydration levels for all
residents, recognising the essential role of fluids in promoting overall health and wellbeing.
Hydration practices include:
- Providing access to clean, palatable drinking water at all times
- Encouraging regular fluid intake through daily routines and during meals
- Offering a variety of drinks including water, juice, milk, and warm beverages
- Monitoring fluid intake, especially for residents at risk of dehydration
- Using fluid balance charts when clinically indicated
- Recognising signs of dehydration and responding appropriately
Staff receive training in promoting hydration and supporting residents who may need assistance with
drinking.

Section 8: Food Safety
8. Food Safety
Secure Living Support Services Ltd ensures that all food provided to residents is safe, hygienic, and
prepared in compliance with relevant legislation and best practice standards.
Food safety measures include:
- Adhering to HSE and HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) guidelines
- Ensuring all staff involved in food preparation complete certified food hygiene training
- Maintaining kitchen cleanliness, proper food storage, and correct food handling procedures
- Monitoring food temperatures during preparation, storage, and serving
- Regular inspections of food preparation areas
- Immediate action in response to food-related incidents, including suspected contamination or food
poisoning
The policy aims to minimise the risk of infection or allergic reaction and uphold the highest standards
of resident care.

Section 9: Monitoring & Review
9. Monitoring & Review
Secure Living Support Services Ltd regularly monitors and evaluates its nutrition and hydration
practices to ensure compliance with standards, identify areas for improvement, and uphold the
wellbeing of residents.
Monitoring activities include:
- Regular audits of meal quality, dietary records, and fluid balance documentation
- Feedback from residents, families, and staff on meal satisfaction
- Incident reporting of any food-related issues or near misses
- Review of compliance with dietary plans and special needs
- Ongoing staff competency checks and refresher training
This policy is reviewed annually or sooner if significant changes in regulation, practice, or resident
needs occur.

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Nutrition & Hydration Policy
Section 10: Policy Review
10. Policy Review
This Nutrition & Hydration Policy will be reviewed annually by the management team of Secure
Living Support Services Ltd or sooner if required, to reflect changes in legislation, best practice
guidelines, or service user needs.
The review process will include:
- Audit findings and feedback from staff and residents
- Updates to relevant nutritional or food safety regulations
- Incident and complaint trends related to food, fluid, or mealtime practices
- Changes in resident population or dietary requirements
The reviewed policy will be approved by the Registered Provider and communicated to all relevant
staff. Superseded versions will be archived and retained according to record management
procedures.
