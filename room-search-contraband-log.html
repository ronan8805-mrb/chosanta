Room Search & Contraband Log

HIQA Standard 3 · S.I. 674/2017 · Children First Act 2015 · UNCRC Article 16

A room search is an intrusion on a child's right to privacy (UNCRC Article 16). It must only be conducted where there is a reasonable belief that there is an immediate risk to the safety of the child or others, or that prohibited items are present. Every search must be authorised, documented on this form, and reviewed by the PIC.

⚠ Rights-Based Approach: Room searches must be proportionate, respectful, and conducted with the minimum intrusion necessary. The child must be informed before or immediately after the search (unless doing so would increase risk). Personal items such as diaries, letters, and photographs must not be read or removed unless they constitute evidence of harm. Tusla ACIMS inspectors will review this log for proportionality.
1 Grounds for Search

A search may only be authorised on one or more of the following grounds:

  • Reasonable suspicion of illegal substances (drugs, alcohol where prohibited)
  • Reasonable suspicion of weapons or sharp objects capable of causing harm
  • Reasonable suspicion of stolen property
  • Concern about self-harm materials (ligatures, blades, hoarded medication)
  • Concern about fire-setting materials (lighters, accelerants)
  • Concern about items facilitating exploitation (unregistered phones, cash of unknown origin)
  • Return from absence / absconding where risk is assessed as high
