Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Infection Prevention and Control Policy
Section 1: Purpose
1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish clear infection prevention and control (IPC) standards and
procedures across Secure Living Support Services Ltd. This is to protect residents, staff, visitors,
and contractors from the transmission of infectious diseases in accordance with HIQA National
Standards, the Health Act 2007, and HSE best practice guidelines.
This policy outlines preventive and responsive measures to:
- Minimise risk of infection in care settings
- Promote hygiene and sanitation
- Support outbreak management
- Ensure staff understand their roles in maintaining infection control at all times

Section 2: Scope
2. Scope
This policy applies to all Secure Living Support Services Ltd locations, staff, residents, contractors,
agency personnel, and visitors.
It encompasses all aspects of infection prevention and control in:
- Residential units and supported living facilities
- Common areas, kitchens, laundry, and bathrooms
- Medical and personal care delivery
- Transport and external activities
The policy is relevant to all infectious risks, including but not limited to:
- Respiratory illnesses (e.g. COVID-19, influenza)
- Gastrointestinal infections (e.g. Norovirus)
- Bloodborne viruses (e.g. Hepatitis B/C)
- Antibiotic-resistant organisms (e.g. MRSA, CPE)

Section 3: Responsibilities
3. Responsibilities
Infection prevention and control is a shared responsibility across all levels of the organisation.
3.1 Registered Provider:
- Ensure the organisation has effective IPC systems and resources
- Oversee compliance with HIQA standards and statutory requirements
3.2 Person in Charge (PIC):
- Implement IPC policy in daily operations
- Ensure all staff receive training and updates
- Monitor infection risks, outbreaks, and cleaning protocols
- Report serious incidents to HIQA where required
3.3 Staff Members:
- Follow IPC protocols including hand hygiene, PPE, and safe waste disposal
- Report symptoms, risks, or breaches immediately
- Attend IPC training and refresher sessions
3.4 Residents and Visitors:
- Encouraged to maintain personal hygiene
- Comply with outbreak precautions and visitor policies

Infection Prevention and Control Policy
Section 4: Key Measures
4. Key Measures
To ensure effective infection prevention and control, Secure Living Support Services Ltd implements
the following measures consistently across all services:
4.1 Hand Hygiene:
- Hand washing facilities with soap and water available at key locations
- Use of alcohol-based hand sanitiser in communal and care areas
- Staff and residents educated on correct hand hygiene technique
4.2 Use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):
- PPE provided based on risk assessment (e.g., gloves, aprons, masks, visors)
- Safe donning, doffing, and disposal procedures in place
- Training in PPE use for all relevant staff
4.3 Cleaning and Disinfection:
- Daily cleaning schedules with increased frequency during outbreaks
- Approved disinfectants used in high-touch and high-risk areas
- Deep cleaning protocols following suspected or confirmed infections
4.4 Waste Management:
- Segregation of clinical and domestic waste
- Safe disposal procedures including secure sharps bins and yellow bags
- Staff training in waste handling protocols
4.5 Laundry Procedures:
- Separate handling of soiled and clean linen
- High-temperature washing as appropriate
- Staff to use gloves and aprons during handling

Section 5: Training
5. Training
Training is essential to ensure that all staff understand infection risks, control measures, and their
role in maintaining a safe environment.
5.1 Mandatory Training:
- All new staff must complete infection prevention and control training during their induction
- This includes standard precautions, hand hygiene, PPE use, waste disposal, and outbreak
response
5.2 Ongoing Training:
- Refresher training is required every 2 years, or earlier if policy changes or after outbreaks
- Training is tailored to staff roles (e.g., cleaning staff, healthcare assistants, kitchen staff)
5.3 Record-Keeping:
- Attendance is logged in the Staff Training Register
- The Person in Charge is responsible for monitoring compliance
5.4 Evaluation:
- Training outcomes are reviewed regularly and revised where necessary to align with national
guidance and HIQA requirements

Section 6: Cleaning Protocols
6. Cleaning Protocols
Effective environmental cleaning is critical to minimising infection risk across all facilities. Secure
Living Support Services Ltd implements strict cleaning protocols in line with HSE and HIQA
standards.
6.1 Daily Cleaning:
- General cleaning of all communal areas, bedrooms, bathrooms, kitchens, and corridors
- High-touch surfaces (e.g. door handles, switches, handrails) cleaned at least twice daily
6.2 Deep Cleaning:
- Conducted weekly in high-traffic areas or more frequently during outbreak periods
- Includes skirting boards, walls, under furniture, and sanitisation of soft furnishings
6.3 Disinfectant Use:
- Only HSE-approved cleaning agents and disinfectants are used
- Correct dilution and contact times are adhered to
- Colour-coded equipment is used to prevent cross-contamination
6.4 Cleaning Logs:
- All cleaning activities are logged with date, time, area, and initials of staff member
- Logs are reviewed weekly by the PIC for compliance
6.5 Staff Training:
- Cleaning staff are trained in infection prevention, use of PPE, and cleaning techniques

Section 7: PPE and Supplies
7. PPE and Supplies
The consistent availability and correct use of personal protective equipment (PPE) and infection
control supplies is vital to preventing transmission of infectious agents within residential care
settings.
7.1 PPE Types and Usage:
- Gloves, aprons, surgical masks, FFP2/FFP3 respirators, face shields, and goggles
- PPE is worn according to task-specific risk assessments and HSE guidance
- Correct donning and doffing techniques are taught and monitored
7.2 Storage and Access:
- PPE is stored in designated, easily accessible clean areas
- Stock levels are checked weekly and reordered proactively
7.3 Supply Chain:
- Infection control supplies (hand gel, wipes, sharps bins, disinfectants) are included in standard
orders
- Emergency stock reserves are maintained onsite in case of outbreaks
7.4 Monitoring:
- PIC is responsible for overseeing stock levels and PPE compliance
- Any supply issues or PPE misuse are escalated immediately
7.5 Environmental Considerations:
- Disposable PPE is disposed of in accordance with healthcare waste protocols
- Reusable items (e.g., goggles) are sanitised after each use

Section 8: Outbreak Response
8. Outbreak Response
Secure Living Support Services Ltd has clear procedures for identifying, managing, and containing
infectious outbreaks to protect the health and safety of residents, staff, and visitors.
8.1 Identification:
- All suspected outbreaks (e.g., two or more residents/staff with similar symptoms) are reported to
the Person in Charge
- Clinical guidance is sought immediately from a GP or HSE Public Health
8.2 Isolation Measures:
- Affected individuals are isolated or cohorted as appropriate
- Dedicated staff and equipment are assigned to affected areas
- Entry and exit protocols are enforced
8.3 Communication:
- Families, staff, and health authorities are informed of the outbreak
- Transparent updates are provided throughout the outbreak
8.4 Enhanced Infection Control:
- Frequency of cleaning and disinfection is increased
- Additional PPE and hand hygiene measures are introduced
- Non-essential activities and visits are suspended
8.5 Documentation:
- A full outbreak report is maintained including timeline, actions taken, and outcomes
- Post-outbreak debriefing and review inform future improvements

Section 9: Monitoring & Audit
9. Monitoring & Audit
Ongoing monitoring and structured audits are conducted to ensure consistent application of infection
prevention measures and to identify areas for improvement.
9.1 Routine Monitoring:
- Daily checks of hand hygiene facilities, PPE availability, and cleanliness
- PIC and team leaders conduct walk-through reviews and log compliance observations
9.2 Scheduled Audits:
- Formal IPC audits conducted monthly using a standard checklist
- Focus on cleaning protocols, equipment sanitation, staff compliance, and PPE usage
- Results reviewed by management and discussed during governance meetings
9.3 Outcomes & Actions:
- Audit findings documented, dated, and signed
- Action plans created for any non-compliance with assigned responsibility and deadlines
- Progress on action plans monitored until resolution
9.4 Continuous Improvement:
- Learning from audits and incidents is integrated into future training and protocols
- Residents and staff are encouraged to provide feedback and suggestions
- Results are reported to the Registered Provider and retained for inspection by HIQA

Section 10: Policy Review and Continuous Improvement
10. Policy Review and Continuous Improvement
This Infection Prevention and Control Policy is a living document that evolves in line with current
evidence, regulation, and operational learning.
10.1 Review Cycle:
- The policy will be formally reviewed at least annually by the Registered Provider and Person in
Charge
- Interim reviews will be triggered by HIQA updates, outbreaks, new legislation, or audit findings
10.2 Feedback Integration:
- Staff, residents, and families may contribute feedback via governance meetings or suggestion
processes
- Lessons learned from incidents and audits are documented and used to inform revisions
10.3 Version Control:
- All versions of the policy are dated, signed, and archived for reference
- The most recent version is clearly marked and accessible to all staff
10.4 Ongoing Improvement:
- The organisation remains committed to aligning its IPC standards with best practice and regulatory
expectations
