Keyworking Session Log

HIQA Standards 1, 2 & 3 · UNCRC Articles 12 & 13 · S.I. 259/1995

Keyworking is central to the child's experience in residential care. HIQA inspectors review keyworker records for regularity (minimum fortnightly), quality of engagement, evidence of goal-setting, and proof that the child's voice is captured in their own words. Sessions should be recorded promptly and filed in the child's individual care file.

📣 Child Voice Principle (HIQA Standard 1): The child's views must be recorded in their own language. Inspectors will look for direct quotes, not staff interpretations. Where a child cannot or chooses not to participate, this must be documented with the reason and an alternative offered.
