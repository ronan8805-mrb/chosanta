1. PURPOSE
To ensure the safe and efficient evacuation of all occupants in the event of an emergency, minimising risk to
life and enabling rapid response by emergency services.
2. SCOPE
Applies to all staff, residents/service users, contractors, and visitors present in the premises at any time.
3. EMERGENCY TYPES COVERED
- Fire or Smoke
- Gas Leak
- Bomb Threat
- Medical Emergency
- Severe Weather / Flood
- Security Threat or Intruder
4. ALARM SYSTEM
- Type: [Fire alarm/manual call point/smoke detection]
- Audible & visual alarms located throughout the premises.
- All staff trained to recognise and respond immediately.
5. EVACUATION PROCEDURE
1. Raise the Alarm
   - Activate the nearest fire alarm point or call out loudly.
   - Call 999/112 and state the emergency type, location, and any hazards.
2. Immediate Action
   - Stop all activities.

   - Leave belongings behind.
   - Assist residents/service users to evacuate, prioritising those with mobility or sensory impairments.
3. Use of Exits
   - Follow illuminated exit signs to the nearest safe exit.
   - Do not use lifts.
   - Close doors behind you to slow the spread of fire/smoke.
4. Assembly Point
   - Proceed to: [Specify Assembly Point]
   - Stay in a group and await instructions.
5. Roll Call
   - Person in Charge or Fire Warden to take register.
   - Report anyone missing to emergency services immediately.
6. RESPONSIBILITIES
- Person in Charge / Fire Warden:
  - Lead evacuation and ensure building is cleared.
  - Take roll call.
  - Liaise with emergency services.
- Staff:
  - Follow evacuation routes.
  - Assist service users.
  - Do not re-enter the building until declared safe.
- Visitors:
  - Must be escorted to the assembly point by staff.
7. ASSISTANCE FOR PERSONS WITH DISABILITIES
- Assign staff "buddies" to assist individuals with mobility or sensory needs.
- Ensure evacuation chairs or mobility aids are positioned at stairwells.
8. TRAINING & DRILLS
- Evacuation drills every 6 months.
- New staff trained during induction.
- Records maintained.
