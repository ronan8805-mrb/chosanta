Statement of Purpose & Admissions

1 Statement of Purpose

Silverwings maintains a comprehensive Statement of Purpose that accurately describes its aims, objectives, and services, in compliance with Child Care Regulations, HIQA, and Tusla requirements.

  • Reflects ethos, philosophy, and care model; defines capacity, age range (6–18), gender mix, and admission criteria
  • Describes physical layout, staffing arrangements, and care/education/health/spiritual supports
  • Reviewed annually by PIC and Registered Provider; updated after any significant change
  • Shared with Tusla, HIQA, staff, and professionals; child-friendly version available
2 Admissions Policy

A transparent, fair, and structured admissions process ensures placements are appropriate, risk-assessed, and compatible with existing residents.

  • Admissions based on detailed impact risk assessment, information sharing, and matching to existing residents
  • Offered only where centre can meet needs, staffing/safety capacity is sufficient, and existing group unaffected
  • Emergency admissions only with relevant information and regional placement team oversight

Admission Procedure:

  • Receive formal Tusla referral and care plan → conduct pre-admission impact risk assessment
  • Share Statement of Purpose and house rules (age-appropriate) → schedule admission meeting
  • Assign keyworker → prepare individual placement plan → update admissions register
  • Provide orientation: fire safety, rights, complaints process

Safeguarding, Rights & Emotional Support

3 Safeguarding & Child Protection

Zero-tolerance approach to abuse, compliant with Children First Act 2015. All staff are mandated persons.

  • DLP: Person in Charge; Deputy DLP nominated and trained
  • All staff receive Children First training; report concerns immediately to DLP
  • Written Mandated Reports submitted through the Tusla portal
  • DLP ensures all concerns logged, reported, and responded to per Tusla guidelines
4 Service User Rights & Advocacy
  • Child-friendly Rights Charter provided to all children
  • Access to EPIC and other advocacy services
  • Involvement in care planning, reviews, and feedback via meetings and questionnaires
  • PIC ensures all children receive copy of rights; keyworkers discuss regularly
5 Emotional & Psychological Support
  • Keyworking sessions for ongoing emotional check-ins
  • Referral to external professionals (psychologist, CAMHS) when needed
  • Therapeutic crisis support available
  • Warm, consistent environment built on trust; PIC ensures timely referrals
