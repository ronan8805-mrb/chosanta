Visitor Log & Access Register

HIQA Standard 3 & 5 · S.I. 674/2017 · Data Protection / GDPR

All visitors to the residential centre must sign in and out. No visitor may have unsupervised access to children without authorisation from the PIC. This log is subject to inspection by Tusla ACIMS and must be stored securely for a minimum of 7 years.

⚠ Safeguarding Rule: No person who has not been Garda vetted may have unsupervised access to any child in the centre. All visitors in contact with children must be supervised by a vetted staff member at all times unless pre-authorised by PIC. This includes contractors, maintenance staff, and family members where court-ordered supervision is in place.
1 Visitor Access Conditions
  • Pre-arranged visits: Social workers, GALs, therapists, EPIC advocates — check ID, log entry, no supervision required if professionally vetted
  • Family / contact visits: Must comply with care plan access arrangements — check for court restrictions before admitting
  • Contractors / maintenance: Must be supervised at all times — no unsupervised access to any area where children are present
  • Tusla / HIQA inspectors: Always admitted — verify ID, log entry, inform PIC immediately
  • An Garda Síochána: Admitted — verify ID and warrant (if applicable), log entry, inform PIC and Director
  • Unscheduled / unexpected visitors: Refer to PIC before admitting — do not admit if identity or purpose cannot be verified
