Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy
1. Purpose
This policy ensures that all residents are protected from abuse, neglect, and exploitation. It is based
on the National Policy "Safeguarding Vulnerable Persons at Risk of Abuse" (HSE, 2014) and aligns
with HIQA standards and the Health Act 2007.
2. Scope
Applies to all staff, contractors, volunteers, and management within Secure Living Support Services
Ltd.
3. Definition of Abuse
Abuse may be physical, emotional, sexual, financial, institutional, or neglect. Any suspicion,
disclosure, or evidence of abuse must be acted on immediately.
4. Responsibilities
* All staff are mandatory reporters.
* The Person in Charge (PIC) is the Designated Safeguarding Officer.
* The Governance Officer (Ian Devine) oversees all safeguarding reviews and audits.
5. Reporting Procedures
Step 1: If abuse is suspected or disclosed, report to the PIC immediately.
Step 2: The PIC logs the concern in the Safeguarding Log and notifies the HSE Safeguarding and
Protection Team.
Step 3: Immediate safety plans are implemented for the resident.
Step 4: The issue is investigated in line with HSE Safeguarding procedures and HIQA guidance.
6. Training
All staff receive mandatory safeguarding training during induction and annually. Records of
attendance are kept on file.
7. Support for Residents
Residents are informed of their rights and how to report concerns. Advocacy services are available
upon request.
8. Confidentiality
All reports are handled confidentially, respecting the privacy of all involved, except where disclosure
is required for protection or investigation.
9. External Reporting
Safeguarding concerns may also be reported directly to:
HSE Safeguarding & Protection Team
or
HIQA - (021) 240 9300 / concerns@hiqa.ie
10. Monitoring
The Governance Officer reviews all safeguarding logs monthly. Trends are analysed and reported to
the Board.
Approved by:
Daisy Cannon, Director
Secure Living Support Services Ltd

Staff Recruitment, Vetting & Induction Policy
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting & Induction Policy
1. Purpose
To ensure that all staff employed by Secure Living Support Services Ltd are appropriately qualified,
vetted, and trained to deliver high-quality care to residents, in line with HIQA and Health Act 2007
standards.
2. Scope
This policy applies to all prospective employees, volunteers, contractors, and agency workers.
3. Recruitment Principles
- Transparent and fair recruitment
- No discrimination on the basis of gender, age, disability, religion, or ethnicity
- All roles are publicly advertised with clear job descriptions and qualification requirements
4. Selection Process
Step 1: Application Review
Step 2: Shortlisting based on qualifications and experience
Step 3: Formal interview
Step 4: Reference checks (minimum of 2 professional references)
5. Vetting Requirements
- All staff must be Garda Vetted before employment begins
- International vetting is required for individuals who lived outside Ireland for more than 6 months
- A photo ID, proof of address, and qualifications are required for verification
6. Contracts & Declarations
- All staff sign an employment contract and confidentiality agreement
- A medical declaration of fitness to work is required
7. Induction Programme
All new staff complete an induction that includes:
* Introduction to Secure Living Support Services Ltd and its mission
* Review of HIQA standards and responsibilities
* Fire safety and safeguarding training
* Manual handling and infection control
* Shadowing with a senior staff member
8. Probation
All new staff complete a 6-month probation period with formal review after 3 months.
9. Training Register
A staff training register is maintained and monitored to ensure annual refreshers are completed.
Approved by:
Daisy Cannon, Director
Secure Living Support Services Ltd

=====Policy_12_Service_User_Rights_Advocacy_Secure_Living.pdf=====
Service User Rights & Advocacy Policy
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Service User Rights & Advocacy Policy
1. Purpose
To uphold and promote the rights, choices, and dignity of all residents, and to ensure each person
has access to independent advocacy and support.
2. Scope
Applies to all residents and staff within Secure Living Support Services Ltd.
3. Rights of Residents
Each service user has the right to:
- Be treated with dignity and respect
- Privacy in all aspects of personal care
- Make choices about their daily life, including meals, routines, and relationships
- Participate in decisions about their care and support
- Access their own personal records
- Make complaints without fear of reprisal
- Live free from abuse, neglect, or exploitation
4. Advocacy Support
- Residents may appoint a family member, friend, or professional advocate to speak on their behalf
- Staff assist residents in accessing external advocacy services if requested
- Where necessary, the National Advocacy Service (NAS) may be contacted
5. Communication
- Staff ensure that all residents understand their rights, using accessible formats and tools
- Residents are encouraged to express their views through house meetings, keyworker sessions,
and complaints processes
6. Informed Consent
- Residents are involved in all decision-making relating to their care
- Informed consent is obtained for all assessments, interventions, and support plans
7. Complaints & Appeals
- A clear complaints policy is in place and communicated to all residents
- Complaints are recorded, investigated, and resolved in a timely and fair manner
8. Monitoring
- Residents' rights and advocacy use are monitored through internal audits and resident feedback
9. Policy Review
This policy is reviewed annually or when significant changes in legislation occur
Approved by:
Daisy Cannon, Director
Secure Living Support Services Ltd

Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Social, Recreational & Community Participation Policy
1. Purpose
To promote meaningful participation in social, recreational, and community-based activities for all
residents, enhancing quality of life, wellbeing, and inclusion.
2. Scope
Applies to all residents and staff at Secure Living Support Services Ltd.
3. Principles
- Each resident has the right to access and participate in community life
- Activities are person-centred, culturally appropriate, and led by individual interests
- Social inclusion reduces isolation and promotes independence
4. Assessment & Planning
- Activities are included in the resident's Personal Plan
- Preferences are identified through keyworker discussions, family input, and resident meetings
- Participation goals are monitored and reviewed regularly
5. Examples of Activities
- Daily leisure (music, art, games)
- Community trips (cinema, library, shops, parks)
- Supported employment or volunteering
- Religious and cultural events
- Holidays and day trips
6. Support & Supervision
- Staff support is provided based on individual risk assessments
- Ratios are determined by needs and support levels
- Independence is promoted wherever safe
7. Resources & Transport
- Secure Living maintains vehicles and travel supports for outings
- Expenses may be supported where appropriate through activity budgets
8. Inclusion & Rights
- Residents are free to refuse participation
- No resident is excluded on the basis of ability, background, or support needs
9. Monitoring
- Engagement logs and resident feedback guide improvements
- Outcomes are recorded in the resident's file
10. Review
Policy is reviewed annually or based on resident needs.
Approved by:
Daisy Cannon, Director
Secure Living Support Services Ltd

Policy 1: Statement of Purpose Policy
Centre Name: Silverwings Children’s Residential Centre
Location: Sillogue, Monasterboice, Co. Louth
1. Purpose
This policy sets out the requirement for Silverwings Children’s Residential Centre to maintain and review a clear Statement of Purpose that accurately describes the aims, objectives, and services provided by the centre. It ensures compliance with the Child Care (Standards in Children’s Residential Centres) Regulations, HIQA standards, and Tusla registration requirements.
2. Scope
This policy applies to the Registered Provider, Person in Charge (PIC), all staff, social workers, visiting professionals, and other stakeholders involved in the delivery of care at Silverwings.
3. Policy Statement
Silverwings will maintain a comprehensive Statement of Purpose that:
- Accurately reflects the ethos, philosophy, and care model used
- Defines the centre’s capacity, age range, gender mix, and admission criteria
- Describes the physical layout of the premises and staffing arrangements
- Outlines the care, education, health, emotional, and spiritual supports provided
- Identifies governance and quality assurance structures

The Statement of Purpose will be:
- Reviewed annually by the Person in Charge and Registered Provider
- Updated immediately following any significant change (e.g., staffing, services, physical premises)
- Shared with Tusla, HIQA, staff, and relevant professionals
- Made available in a child-friendly version for young people in the centre
4. Roles and Responsibilities
- The Person in Charge is responsible for drafting, reviewing, and disseminating the Statement of Purpose.
- The Registered Provider must approve and ensure the Statement reflects actual service provision.
- Staff are required to be familiar with the contents and implement practices aligned to the stated purpose.
5. Monitoring and Review
This policy will be reviewed annually or sooner if required. Changes will be documented, version-controlled, and dated appropriately.

=====Policy_2_Admissions_Policy.docx=====
Policy 2: Admissions Policy
Centre Name: Silverwings Children’s Residential Centre
Location: Sillogue, Monasterboice, Co. Louth
1. Purpose
To establish a transparent, fair, and structured admissions process that ensures the placement of children into Silverwings is appropriate, risk-assessed, and meets the child’s needs while ensuring compatibility with other residents.
2. Scope
This policy applies to all referrals, emergency and planned admissions, and transitions involving Silverwings Children’s Residential Centre. It must be followed by the Registered Provider, Person in Charge, social workers, and placement officers.
3. Policy Statement
Silverwings admits children aged 6–18 years who require residential care in line with Tusla placement agreements. All admissions are based on a detailed impact risk assessment, information sharing with professionals, and matching to existing residents.

Admission is offered only where:
- The centre can meet the assessed needs of the young person
- Staffing and safety capacity is sufficient
- The existing resident group will not be adversely affected

Emergency admissions will only be considered with relevant information and regional placement team oversight.
4. Admission Procedure
- Receive formal referral and care plan from Tusla
- Conduct pre-admission impact risk assessment
- Share Statement of Purpose and house rules with the young person (age-appropriate)
- Schedule admission meeting involving key staff, social worker, and family (where applicable)
- Assign a keyworker and prepare an individual placement plan
- Update admissions register and notify Tusla registration team
- Provide orientation to the centre, including fire safety, rights, and complaints process
5. Roles and Responsibilities
- The Person in Charge coordinates all admissions, ensures all documents are obtained, and placement is suitable.
- The Registered Provider oversees governance of matching and compliance with registration standards.
- All staff are required to support induction, welcome the child, and contribute to placement stability.
6. Monitoring and Review
This policy will be reviewed annually or earlier following any placement disruption, inspection recommendation, or regulatory update.

Policy 3: Safeguarding and Child Protection Policy
Centre Name: Silverwings Children’s Residential Centre
Location: Sillogue, Monasterboice, Co. Louth
1. Purpose
To ensure all children are protected from harm, abuse, and neglect in accordance with the Children First Act 2015, and that all staff are clear on their roles as mandated persons.
2. Scope
This policy applies to all staff, the Person in Charge, the Registered Provider, and any visiting professionals or volunteers.
3. Policy Statement
Silverwings recognises its duty of care and legal obligations to safeguard all children. We commit to a zero-tolerance approach to abuse and will respond swiftly and appropriately to all concerns, disclosures, or allegations.

The Designated Liaison Person (DLP) is the Person in Charge. The Deputy DLP is nominated and trained appropriately.

All staff:
- Receive Children First training
- Are familiar with reporting thresholds
- Report concerns immediately to the DLP
- Submit written Mandated Reports through the Tusla portal
4. Responsibilities
- The DLP ensures all concerns are logged, reported, and responded to per Tusla guidelines.
- All staff are responsible for observing, documenting, and acting on any signs of abuse or neglect.
- The Registered Provider ensures safeguarding is embedded in governance and training is current.
5. Monitoring and Review
This policy is reviewed annually or after any safeguarding incident.
