Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy - Section 1: Purpose
1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that Secure Living Support Services Ltd provides a safe and
protective environment for all adults using its services, particularly those who may be vulnerable to
abuse, neglect, or exploitation.
This policy sets out the organisation's commitment to promoting the welfare of every individual in our
care and outlines clear procedures for the prevention, identification, reporting, and management of
safeguarding concerns.
This policy aims to:
- Prevent harm by embedding a culture of vigilance, respect, and protection.
- Ensure early detection of abuse, neglect, or exploitation through clear staff guidance and open
communication.
- Promote person-centred care, respecting the dignity, autonomy, and rights of each individual.
- Comply with all legal obligations, including the Health Act 2007, the National Standards for
Residential Services, and the HSE's Safeguarding Vulnerable Persons at Risk of Abuse Policy
(2014).
- Support staff to respond effectively to any safeguarding concerns through training, supervision,
and clear reporting structures.

Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy - Section 2: Scope
2. Scope
This policy applies to all services, locations, staff, and volunteers operating under Secure Living
Support Services Ltd where adults at risk may be receiving support, care, or accommodation. It is
designed to ensure a consistent, safe, and lawful approach to safeguarding across all areas of

- All residential or supported living settings provided by Secure Living Support Services Ltd
- All full-time, part-time, relief, agency, and voluntary staff

  - Intellectual or physical disabilities
  - Mental health needs
  - Age-related vulnerabilities
  - Communication difficulties
  - Complex or high-support needs
2.2 Does Not Apply to:
- Children or young people under 18 years (covered under the Child Safeguarding Policy)
- Visitors, families, or external professionals unless directly involved in service delivery or under
safeguarding concern
2.3 Responsibility:
It is the duty of every person working or volunteering in Secure Living Support Services Ltd to
adhere to this policy and to report any safeguarding concern, no matter how minor it may seem.

=====Safeguarding_Policy_Section3_Definitions_of_Abuse.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy - Section 3: Definitions of Abuse
3. Definitions: Abuse
Abuse is defined as 'any act, or failure to act, which results in harm, potential harm, or exploitation of
a vulnerable adult.'
It may be a single act, repeated actions, or occur over a period of time, and can be intentional or
unintentional.
Abuse can happen in any setting - including at home, in the community, or in care environments -
and may be carried out by anyone: family, friends, staff, peers, strangers, or professionals.
3.1 Types of Abuse
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises the following forms of abuse:
- Physical abuse: Hitting, slapping, pushing, kicking, misuse of medication, restraint, or
force-feeding.
- Sexual abuse: Non-consensual sexual activity, inappropriate touching, exploitation, or indecent
exposure.
- Psychological/emotional abuse: Intimidation, threats, humiliation, isolation, verbal abuse, or
coercive control.
- Financial or material abuse: Theft, fraud, misuse of funds, pressure around wills or property, or
denial of access to money.
- Neglect or acts of omission: Failure to provide food, hygiene, medical care, or assistance with
basic needs.
- Institutional abuse: Poor care practices, rigid routines, lack of person-centred planning, or
disrespect of rights.
- Discriminatory abuse: Harassment, slurs, or unequal treatment based on age, gender, race,
religion, disability, or sexual orientation.
- Domestic abuse: Patterns of controlling, coercive, threatening behaviour between intimate partners
or family members.
- Self-neglect: When a person neglects their own health, hygiene, or environment, putting
themselves at risk.
- Cyber abuse: Online threats, grooming, bullying, scamming, or exploitation via digital devices.

=====Safeguarding_Policy_Section4_Responsibilities.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy - Section 4: Responsibilities
4. Responsibilities
Everyone involved in Secure Living Support Services Ltd has a duty to promote a culture of
safeguarding and to respond appropriately to any concern, disclosure, or incident of abuse.
4.1 Organisational Responsibility (Registered Provider & Director)
- Ensure safeguarding is embedded in all policies, procedures, and governance systems.
- Appoint a Designated Safeguarding Officer (DSO) for the service.
- Maintain oversight of all safeguarding reports and ensure appropriate action is taken.
- Ensure systems are in place for staff training, reporting, and record-keeping.
- Monitor and review safeguarding trends through governance meetings and audits.
4.2 Designated Safeguarding Officer (DSO)
- Receive and respond to all concerns or disclosures of abuse.
- Assess immediate risks and take protective action where required.
- Liaise with the HSE Safeguarding and Protection Team.
- Complete required documentation (e.g. Preliminary Screening Form).
- Support staff through the reporting process and provide debriefing if necessary.
4.3 Person in Charge (PIC)
- Promote safeguarding as part of daily leadership and supervision.
- Ensure care plans include safeguarding needs and risk assessments.
- Monitor for institutional risk factors (e.g. poor staffing, over-reliance on restraint).
- Escalate concerns to the DSO without delay.
4.4 All Staff & Volunteers
- Be alert to signs of abuse, neglect, or harm.
- Respond respectfully and calmly to any disclosure.
- Report concerns immediately to the DSO or PIC.
- Maintain confidentiality and avoid discussing allegations with others.
- Attend all safeguarding training and refreshers.

=====Safeguarding_Policy_Section5_Reporting_Procedures.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy - Section 5: Reporting Procedures
5. Reporting Procedures
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to a clear, timely, and confidential reporting
process when any safeguarding concern arises. All staff have a duty of care to act without delay and
in the best interest of the vulnerable person.
5.1 Recognising and Responding to Abuse
If a staff member witnesses, suspects, or receives a disclosure of abuse, they must:
- Stay calm and listen.
- Reassure the person they are being taken seriously.
- Avoid asking leading questions.
- Do not promise confidentiality - explain the obligation to report.
- Ensure the person is safe and supported.
5.2 Internal Reporting Steps
1. Report immediately to the Designated Safeguarding Officer (DSO) or Person in Charge (PIC).
2. The DSO will assess immediate risk and determine if emergency services or Gardaí are required.
3. A Safeguarding Concern Form must be completed within 24 hours.
4. The DSO may complete the Preliminary Screening Form (PSF) and liaise with the HSE
Safeguarding Team.
5. Concerns are escalated as necessary, including to HIQA if there is a serious incident or risk.
5.3 External Reporting
Where applicable, the DSO will liaise with:
- HSE Safeguarding & Protection Team
- HIQA (where abuse involves serious risk or a designated centre)
- An Garda Síochána (for any suspected criminal activity)
- Tusla, if children are affected or present
5.4 Protection from Retaliation
- All reports are handled with strict confidentiality.
- Staff and service users who report in good faith are protected from reprisal under the Protected
Disclosures Act 2014.

Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy - Section 6: Training
6. Training
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises that robust and ongoing safeguarding training is
essential to protect adults at risk, ensure staff confidence, and comply with national standards.

All staff, including relief, agency, and volunteers, must complete mandatory safeguarding training:
- Upon induction, and
- As part of refresher training every 2 years, or more frequently if required.

Training includes:
- Understanding types and signs of abuse
- Responding to disclosures appropriately
- Internal and external reporting pathways
- Legal obligations and whistleblowing protections
- Record-keeping and confidentiality standards
- Human rights, dignity, and autonomy in care
- Case studies and role-play for practical learning
6.3 Specialist Training
Additional safeguarding training is provided for:
- Designated Safeguarding Officers (DSOs)
- Persons in Charge (PICs)
- Staff supporting residents with complex needs, behaviours of concern, or communication
challenges
6.4 Training Records
- Attendance is recorded in individual staff training files and monitored through HR systems.
- Gaps in training are flagged by line managers and must be addressed before unsupervised care
resumes.

=====Safeguarding_Policy_Section7_Support_for_Residents.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy - Section 7: Support for Residents
7. Support for Residents
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to ensuring that every resident feels safe, heard,
respected, and empowered - especially when a safeguarding concern has been raised or abuse is
suspected.
7.1 Person-Centred Support
- All safeguarding responses will prioritise the resident's wishes, dignity, and safety.
- Residents will be treated with compassion and respect, regardless of the circumstances of the
concern.
- Advocacy will be offered to ensure the person's voice is heard, particularly where capacity or
communication barriers exist.
7.2 Immediate Support Actions
When a concern arises:
- The individual will be removed from harm's way (if applicable).
- Emotional reassurance and practical assistance will be provided immediately.
- A named staff member will remain in contact with the individual for continuity.
7.3 Ongoing Safeguarding Support
- Individual safeguarding needs will be built into the resident's personal care plan.
- Where required, residents will be offered access to:
  - Independent advocacy services
  - Psychological or counselling support
  - Legal or social work services
  - Medical assessment and support
7.4 Right to Be Informed
- Residents will be kept informed at every stage of the safeguarding process in a manner
appropriate to their level of understanding.
- Decisions will be explained clearly, with support provided to aid decision-making wherever
possible.

=====Safeguarding_Policy_Section8_Confidentiality.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy - Section 8: Confidentiality
8. Confidentiality
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to protecting the privacy, dignity, and rights of
residents and staff. Safeguarding concerns will be handled with the utmost confidentiality, balanced
with the duty to report and prevent harm.
8.1 Confidential Handling of Safeguarding Information
- Safeguarding information is shared on a need-to-know basis only and only with relevant authorities
(e.g., HSE, HIQA, Gardaí).
- All safeguarding records are stored securely and accessible only to authorised personnel.
- Details of any safeguarding concern are not discussed with other staff, residents, or third parties
unless required by law.
8.2 Disclosure Obligations
- Staff cannot promise absolute confidentiality to a resident or family member if there is a risk of
abuse or harm.
- The designated safeguarding officer (DSO) will determine what information must be shared and
with whom.
8.3 GDPR & Data Protection Compliance
- All safeguarding records are maintained in line with the General Data Protection Regulation
(GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.
- Residents have a right to access their records unless doing so would place them or others at risk.
8.4 Confidentiality Training
- All staff receive training on handling safeguarding concerns confidentially, including appropriate
record-keeping, data access, and escalation.

=====Safeguarding_Policy_Section9_External_Contacts.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults Policy - Section 9: External Contacts
9. External Contacts
Secure Living Support Services Ltd works in partnership with key external agencies to ensure that
all safeguarding concerns are dealt with efficiently, transparently, and in accordance with legal and
regulatory obligations.
9.1 Safeguarding & Protection Teams (HSE)
Each HSE region has a Safeguarding and Protection Team (SPT) responsible for responding to
reports of abuse or serious risk to vulnerable adults.
- Role: Receive referrals, coordinate investigations, support services, and recommend actions.
- All Preliminary Screening Forms (PSFs) are submitted directly to the local SPT.
9.2 Health Information and Quality Authority (HIQA)
- HIQA is notified in the event of serious incidents or ongoing risk in designated centres.
- The Registered Provider or PIC must ensure notifications are submitted via the HIQA portal within
3 working days.
9.3 An Garda Síochána
- If a safeguarding concern involves a criminal offence or serious risk of harm, it must be reported
immediately to Gardaí.
- The DSO or PIC liaises with the local Garda station and supports any investigation.
9.4 Tusla - Child and Family Agency
- If children are involved, either as victims or witnesses, Tusla is notified under Children First
guidelines.
- Reports are made using the Child Protection and Welfare Report Form (CPWRF).
9.5 Other Relevant Contacts
Depending on the case, Secure Living Support Services Ltd may also engage:
- GPs or hospital consultants
- Social workers
- Advocacy agencies (e.g., National Advocacy Service)
- Mental health or disability support services

Section 3: Definitions of Abuse
3. Definitions of Abuse
Abuse is defined as any act, or failure to act, which results in harm, potential harm, or distress to a
vulnerable adult.
Types of abuse include:
- **Physical Abuse:** Hitting, slapping, misuse of medication, restraint, or inappropriate sanctions.
- **Psychological/Emotional Abuse:** Intimidation, threats, humiliation, isolation, or verbal abuse.
- **Sexual Abuse:** Rape, sexual assault, inappropriate touching, or any sexual activity the person
does not or cannot consent to.
- **Financial or Material Abuse:** Theft, fraud, pressure in connection with wills or financial
transactions.
- **Neglect or Acts of Omission:** Ignoring medical or physical care needs, failure to provide access
to necessary services.
- **Discriminatory Abuse:** Harassment or unequal treatment based on age, race, gender, disability,
religion, or sexuality.
- **Institutional Abuse:** Repeated poor practice within care settings that violates a person's dignity,
rights, or wellbeing.
All suspected or actual cases of abuse must be taken seriously and acted upon immediately in line
with this policy.

Section 7: Responding to Allegations
7. Responding to Allegations
All allegations of abuse or neglect must be taken seriously, treated sensitively, and acted upon
immediately. The safety and wellbeing of the vulnerable adult is the primary concern at all times.
7.1 Immediate Response:
- Ensure the person is safe and supported
- Avoid asking leading questions or investigating yourself
- Do not confront the alleged perpetrator
- Report the allegation to the Designated Officer without delay
7.2 Initial Action by Designated Officer:
- Assess risk and determine if emergency intervention is required
- Notify senior management and external safeguarding teams if appropriate
- Ensure accurate records are kept
7.3 Suspension or Reassignment:
- Where the alleged perpetrator is a staff member, they may be temporarily reassigned or
suspended pending investigation
- Actions must be taken in line with the organisation's disciplinary policy and fair procedures
7.4 Protection of the Individual:
- The individual will be offered emotional support and advocacy
- Any action taken will aim to protect their dignity, rights, and confidentiality
7.5 Investigation:
- Internal or external investigations will follow recognised safeguarding protocols and timeframes

Safeguarding & Protection of Vulnerable Adults Policy
Section 9: Consent & Capacity
9. Consent & Capacity
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises that all adults have the right to make their own
decisions, unless it can be demonstrated that they lack the capacity to do so.
9.1 Presumption of Capacity:
- Every adult is presumed to have capacity unless proven otherwise
- Staff must not assume incapacity based on a person's condition, age, appearance, or behaviour
9.2 Gaining Consent:
- Where possible, informed consent must be obtained before any action is taken
- Consent should be specific, informed, voluntary, and documented
- Communication should be tailored to the individual's needs, using accessible formats where
necessary
9.3 Assessment of Capacity:
- Capacity assessments must follow the principles of the Assisted Decision-Making (Capacity) Act
2015
- Decisions must be time- and issue-specific
- An individual may have capacity to make some decisions but not others
9.4 Acting Without Consent:
- If a person is found to lack capacity, decisions must be made in their best interest
- This may include consulting with family, representatives, or advocates
- All actions must be the least restrictive option to achieve the intended purpose
9.5 Safeguarding & Consent:
- Abuse may still occur even if a person consents
- Concerns should always be reported and assessed, especially if coercion or undue influence is
suspected
