Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Complaints Policy & Procedures
Section 1: Purpose
1. Purpose
The purpose of this Complaints Policy & Procedures is to ensure that all service users, their families,
and representatives have access to a clear, fair, and transparent process for expressing
dissatisfaction with any aspect of the service provided by Secure Living Support Services Ltd.
This policy promotes a culture of listening, learning, and continuous improvement, and ensures that
complaints are handled promptly, respectfully, and without prejudice. It reflects national standards
and regulatory requirements, including the Health Act 2007 and HIQA's guidance on complaints
management.

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Complaints Policy & Procedures
Section 2: Scope
2. Scope
This policy applies to all complaints raised by or on behalf of:
- Residents and service users of Secure Living Support Services Ltd
- Family members, legal guardians, or advocates acting on their behalf
- Staff, volunteers, and members of the public who interact with the service
The policy covers all aspects of care, support, communication, behaviour, facilities, and
organisational conduct.
It does not cover grievances between staff (which are addressed through internal HR policies) or
legal proceedings that are already underway.

3. Policy Statement

===== Complaints_Policy_Section3_Guiding_Principles.pdf =====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Complaints Policy - Section 3: Guiding Principles
3. Guiding Principles
The following principles guide the handling of all complaints within Secure Living Support Services
Ltd and ensure that the complaints process is transparent, accessible, and respectful of all parties
involved.
1. Rights-Based
All individuals have the right to express dissatisfaction without fear of adverse consequences. The
complaints process is underpinned by the principles of fairness, equality, and the right to be heard.
2. Person-Centred
The needs, wishes, and preferences of the person making the complaint are central to the process.
Wherever possible, complaints are addressed in a way that respects each individual's
communication needs, culture, and autonomy.
3. Accessibility
Information about how to make a complaint is clearly displayed and available in appropriate formats.
Complaints can be made verbally, in writing, anonymously, or with the support of an advocate.
4. Timeliness
Complaints are acknowledged within 5 working days and investigated in a prompt, structured, and
efficient manner. Updates are provided to the complainant at appropriate intervals.
5. Fairness & Confidentiality
All complaints are investigated fairly, impartially, and with due respect for the privacy of all
individuals involved. Only those directly involved in handling the complaint have access to related
information.
6. Responsiveness & Improvement
Complaints are seen as an opportunity for learning and service improvement. Patterns or recurring
concerns are monitored and addressed through quality and risk management systems.
7. Non-Retaliation
No individual will be treated unfairly or differently as a result of making or supporting a complaint.
Secure Living Support Services Ltd maintains a strict no retaliation stance and ensures protection
for complainants and witnesses.

===== Complaints_Policy_Section4_Complaints_Procedure.pdf =====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Complaints Policy - Section 4: Complaints Procedure
4. Complaints Procedure
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to resolving complaints in a transparent, fair, and
timely manner. This procedure outlines how complaints will be received, processed, resolved, and
recorded.
Step 1: Receiving a Complaint
- Complaints can be made verbally, in writing, anonymously, or through an advocate.
- All staff are trained to receive complaints and escalate them as appropriate.
Step 2: Acknowledgement
- Formal complaints are acknowledged in writing within 5 working days.
- The acknowledgement includes the name of the assigned Complaints Officer and expected
timelines.
Step 3: Assessment & Investigation
- Complaints Officer conducts a fair and impartial investigation.
- May involve interviews, documentation review, and consultation with the PIC or Registered
Provider.
- Safeguarding measures are applied if required.
Step 4: Resolution & Response
- A formal written response is issued within 30 working days.
- If delays occur, updates are provided every 20 working days.
- The response outlines findings, actions taken, and appeal options.
Step 5: Appeal Process
- If dissatisfied, the complainant may escalate to the Registered Provider, HIQA, HSE Feedback
Service, or Ombudsman.
- Appeals follow the same fair and transparent approach.
Step 6: Learning & Quality Improvement
- All complaints are recorded in the Complaints Register.
- Reviewed quarterly to identify trends or recurring issues.
- Findings inform service improvements and training where necessary.

===== Complaints_Policy_Section5_Timeframes.pdf =====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Complaints Policy - Section 5: Timeframes
5. Timeframes
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to ensuring that complaints are handled promptly
and within clear, regulated timeframes. These timeframes provide structure and clarity to the
complaints process while ensuring a fair and respectful approach for all parties involved.
5.1 Acknowledgement
- All formal complaints will be acknowledged in writing within 5 working days of receipt.
- The acknowledgement will include:
  - The name and contact details of the Complaints Officer.
  - An outline of the process.
  - Expected timeframe for resolution.
5.2 Investigation and Resolution
- The investigation will be completed, and a written response issued within 30 working days where
possible.
- If the complaint cannot be resolved within 30 working days, the complainant will:
  - Be provided with a written interim update explaining the delay.
  - Receive further written updates every 20 working days thereafter until the complaint is resolved.
5.3 Appeal Process
- Appeals raised by the complainant should be acknowledged within 5 working days.
- A final response to the appeal should be issued within 20 working days of receipt, unless extended
due to complexity.
5.4 Closure
- A complaint is considered closed when:
  - The complainant accepts the written outcome.
  - The appeal process has concluded.
  - The complainant is advised of their right to escalate externally (e.g., HIQA, Ombudsman).
All timelines are monitored and recorded in the Complaints Register and reviewed regularly by the
Governance Officer to ensure compliance and accountability.

===== Complaints_Policy_Section6_Complaints_Log.pdf =====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Complaints Policy - Section 6: Complaints Log & Record-Keeping
6. Complaints Log & Record-Keeping
Secure Living Support Services Ltd maintains a central Complaints Log to record, track, and monitor
all complaints made by or on behalf of residents. This ensures transparency, accountability, and full
compliance with Regulation 34 of the Health Act 2007.
6.1 Complaints Log
- Maintained by the Complaints Officer.
- Includes: complaint reference number, date received, complainant (or anonymous), summary of
issue, staff assigned, actions, communications, status (open/closed), and notes on escalation.
- Stored in electronic and hard copy formats; access is limited to authorised staff.
6.2 Confidentiality and GDPR Compliance
- All records handled under GDPR and Data Protection Acts.
- Personal data is redacted or anonymised as appropriate.
- Access restricted to designated personnel only.
6.3 Monitoring and Reporting
- Governance Officer reviews the log quarterly.
- Emerging trends, recurring complaints, or safeguarding risks are identified.
- Summary reports shared with the Director and PIC during governance meetings.
- Complaint outcomes are incorporated into the Quality Improvement Plan.
6.4 Retention Period
- Records retained for a minimum of 7 years in accordance with national guidance.
- Archived complaints are securely stored and available for inspection by HIQA or relevant
authorities.

===== Complaints_Policy_Section7_Procedure.pdf =====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Complaints Policy & Procedures
Section 7: Procedure
7. Procedure
The following steps outline how complaints are managed within Secure Living Support Services Ltd:
Step 1 - Acknowledgement:
- All complaints are acknowledged within 5 working days of receipt.
Step 2 - Assessment:
- The Complaints Officer determines the nature, complexity, and seriousness of the complaint.
- Urgent issues are prioritised and immediate action taken if needed.
Step 3 - Investigation:
- A fair and impartial investigation is carried out, involving interviews, document review, and input
from relevant parties.
- Complainants are kept informed of progress.
Step 4 - Outcome:
- Findings are documented and shared with the complainant.
- Where appropriate, apologies, corrective action, or service improvements are offered.
Step 5 - Appeal:
- If the complainant is dissatisfied, they may request an internal appeal or refer the matter to the
Ombudsman.
Step 6 - Closure:
- Complaints are formally closed when the investigation is complete and all responses have been
issued.
- All records are retained securely for audit and learning purposes.

===== Complaints_Policy_Section7_Anonymous_Complaints.pdf =====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Complaints Policy - Section 7: Anonymous Complaints
7. Anonymous Complaints
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises that some individuals may wish to raise concerns
without revealing their identity. The organisation is committed to ensuring that anonymous
complaints are taken seriously and handled with due care and professionalism, in line with the
principles of fairness, transparency, and risk management.
7.1 Acceptance of Anonymous Complaints
- Anonymous complaints are accepted and reviewed if sufficient information is provided to allow
meaningful investigation.
- The same standards of investigation and review are applied, where possible, as for identified
complaints.
7.2 Limitations
- The ability to investigate or resolve an anonymous complaint may be limited due to:
  - Lack of specific details (dates, people, location).
  - Inability to clarify or follow up with the complainant.
  - Challenges in providing updates or outcomes.
7.3 Safeguarding and Risk Assessment
- If an anonymous complaint raises potential safeguarding issues, the Designated Safeguarding
Person and Governance Officer will review the matter immediately.
- A formal risk assessment will be carried out, and appropriate action taken in line with the
Safeguarding Policy.
7.4 Documentation
- All anonymous complaints are recorded in the Complaints Log with a note indicating the
complainant's identity is unknown.
- The record will include: nature of concern, date received, steps taken, and outcome or reason for
closure.
- These are reviewed for trends or recurring issues.
7.5 Assurance of Non-Retaliation
- Secure Living Support Services Ltd upholds a non-retaliation policy and encourages openness and
transparency.
- Anonymous complaints are viewed as legitimate feedback and may offer valuable insight into
service quality and culture.

===== Complaints_Policy_Section8_Learning_Improvement.pdf =====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Complaints Policy - Section 8: Learning & Improvement
8. Learning & Improvement
Secure Living Support Services Ltd views complaints as a critical tool for service improvement.
Complaints are not seen as a threat but as an opportunity to strengthen the quality of care, enhance
resident outcomes, and support a culture of transparency and responsiveness.
8.1 Governance Oversight
- All complaints (informal and formal) are monitored by the Governance Officer.
- A quarterly complaints report is submitted to the Director and included in governance meetings.
- The report identifies themes, emerging risks, and service areas requiring review.
8.2 Quality Improvement Planning
- Complaint insights inform the Quality Improvement Plan (QIP).
- Actions may include staff training, policy updates, service redesign, or environmental changes.
- The Complaints Officer works with the Person in Charge to implement changes.
8.3 Feedback to Staff and Stakeholders
- Anonymised summaries may be shared during team meetings, training, or induction.
- Residents and families are updated on service changes made in response to feedback, if they
consent.
8.4 Continuous Review
- Complaints Log trends are analysed every 3 months.
- Recurring complaints may trigger a Root Cause Analysis or focused audit.
- Data is included in the annual governance review to support long-term improvements.

Complaints Policy & Procedures
Section 9: Right to Appeal
9. Right to Appeal
If a complainant is not satisfied with the outcome of their complaint, they have the right to appeal the
decision.
Internal Appeal:
- The complainant may request a review of the decision by a senior manager or a designated
appeals officer not previously involved in the complaint.
- The appeal will be conducted impartially, and the outcome will be communicated within 20 working
days.
External Appeal:
- If the complainant remains dissatisfied, they may escalate the issue to external bodies such as:
  - The Ombudsman
  - HIQA (Health Information and Quality Authority)
  - The HSE or other funding body (if applicable)
Complainants are supported and informed throughout the appeal process, and information on how
to access external bodies is readily available in service literature.
