Nutrition & Hydration Policy

Sections 1–4: Purpose, Scope, Responsibilities & Meal Provision

1 Purpose

To ensure that all residents receive safe, nutritious, and adequate food and fluids that promote their health, wellbeing, and dignity, in accordance with HIQA National Standards and HSE guidelines.

2 Scope
  • All staff involved in planning, preparing, delivering, and monitoring nutrition and hydration
  • Meal planning, food safety, nutritional intake monitoring, fluid balance assessment
  • Special dietary needs including allergies, religious/cultural preferences, and medically indicated diets
3 Responsibilities
  • Provider & PIC: Ensure adequate resources, training, and monitoring systems
  • Care Staff: Support residents during meals, observe intake, record food/fluid charts, report concerns
  • Catering Staff: Prepare meals per dietary plans; ensure food safety and hygiene
  • Healthcare Professionals: Assess individual needs, provide care plans, oversee clinical nutrition
  • All Staff: Promote dignity and choice; respect cultural/religious preferences; follow hygiene protocols
4 Meal Provision
  • Three main meals per day with optional snacks and drinks between meals
  • Variety of choices respecting cultural, religious, medical, and personal preferences
  • Menus planned with residents, dietitians, and relevant professionals
  • Texture-modified diets provided as per assessment (soft, pureed)
  • Mealtimes treated as social, dignified occasions with respectful staff support

Sections 5–7: Resident Involvement, Special Diets & Hydration

5 Resident Involvement
  • Residents encouraged to participate in menu planning and give feedback on meal quality
  • Food preference forms completed on admission and reviewed regularly
  • Special occasions, cultural celebrations, and milestones acknowledged in meal offerings
  • Residents may assist in meal preparation or table setting to enhance independence
  • Concerns raised through forums or keyworker discussions; feedback reviewed by management
6 Special Diets
  • Medically prescribed: Diabetic, low sodium, gluten-free
  • Texture-modified: Pureed, minced and moist, soft
  • Cultural/religious: Halal, kosher, vegetarian
  • Allergy/intolerance: Lactose intolerance, nut-free
  • Dietary needs documented in care plans; staff trained on allergy awareness and food handling
  • Kitchen staff involved in understanding and preparing special meals
7 Hydration Management
  • Access to clean, palatable drinking water at all times
  • Regular fluid intake encouraged through daily routines and during meals
  • Variety of drinks offered: water, juice, milk, warm beverages
  • Fluid intake monitored for residents at risk of dehydration; fluid balance charts used when clinically indicated
  • Staff trained in recognising signs of dehydration and responding appropriately

Sections 8–10: Food Safety, Monitoring & Review

8 Food Safety
  • Adhering to HSE and HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) guidelines
  • All staff involved in food preparation complete certified food hygiene training
  • Kitchen cleanliness, proper food storage, and correct handling procedures maintained
  • Food temperatures monitored during preparation, storage, and serving
  • Regular inspections of food preparation areas
  • Immediate action for food-related incidents including suspected contamination or food poisoning
9 Monitoring & Review
  • Regular audits of meal quality, dietary records, and fluid balance documentation
  • Feedback from residents, families, and staff on meal satisfaction
  • Incident reporting of any food-related issues or near misses
  • Review of compliance with dietary plans and special needs
  • Ongoing staff competency checks and refresher training
10 Policy Review
  • Reviewed annually by management or sooner if required
  • Incorporates audit findings, staff/resident feedback, regulatory updates
  • Considers incident and complaint trends related to food, fluid, or mealtime practices
  • Reviewed policy approved by Registered Provider and communicated to all staff
  • Superseded versions archived per record management procedures
