Child Safeguarding Policy

Policy Template Document

1 Purpose

To ensure all children in our care are protected from harm, abuse, and neglect. This policy outlines procedures for recognising and reporting child protection concerns.

2 Scope

Applies to all staff, volunteers, contractors, and any person working on behalf of the organisation who may come into contact with children.

3 Policy Statement

We are committed to safeguarding all children in our service and believe every child has the right to live free from abuse, exploitation, and harm.

4 Definitions
ChildA person under the age of 18
AbuseCan be physical, emotional, sexual, or neglect
Designated Liaison Person (DLP)Staff member appointed to receive and act on child protection concerns
5 Roles and Responsibilities

All Staff:

  • Must report concerns immediately to the DLP
  • Must complete mandatory child protection training

Designated Liaison Person (DLP):

  • Maintains records of all concerns
  • Liaises with Tusla and An Garda Síochána
  • Ensures follow-up and protection measures are in place
6 Recognising Abuse

Signs include:

  • Unexplained injuries or bruises
  • Sudden changes in behaviour or appearance
  • Reluctance to go home or be with specific adults
  • Poor hygiene or signs of neglect
7 Reporting Procedure
  1. Staff member observes or is told about a concern.
  2. Record the concern on the Standard Report Form.
  3. Notify the DLP immediately.
  4. DLP assesses and reports to Tusla if necessary.
  5. Emergency: Contact Gardaí directly.
8 Confidentiality

All concerns are handled sensitively and confidentially. Information is shared only on a need-to-know basis in accordance with legislation.

9 Recruitment and Vetting

All staff must undergo:

  • Garda Vetting
  • Reference checks (minimum of two)
  • Verification of qualifications
10 Training

All staff must complete:

  • Introduction to Children First e-learning
  • Regular refresher training every 2 years
11 Record Keeping

All concerns, disclosures, and actions taken must be documented and stored securely for 7 years, accessible only to DLP and management.

12 Review

This policy is reviewed annually or after any serious child protection incident.

Tusla — Child and Family Agency: If you have concerns about the safety or welfare of a child, contact Tusla. Tel: 01 771 8500 | Web: www.tusla.ie

An Garda Síochána: In an emergency, contact your local Garda station or call 999 / 112.
