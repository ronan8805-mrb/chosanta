1. Purpose
To ensure all children in our care are protected from harm, abuse, and neglect. This policy outlines procedures for recognising, responding to, and reporting child protection concerns.
2. Scope
Applies to all staff, volunteers, contractors, and any person working on behalf of the organisation who may come into contact with children.
3. Policy Statement
We are committed to safeguarding all children in our service and believe every child has the right to live free from abuse, exploitation, and neglect.
4. Definitions
Child: A person under the age of 18
Abuse: Can be physical, emotional, sexual, or neglect
Designated Liaison Person (DLP): Staff member appointed to receive and act on child protection concerns
5. Roles and Responsibilities
All Staff:
- Must report concerns immediately to the DLP
- Must complete mandatory child protection training
DLP:
- Maintains records of all concerns
- Liaises with Tusla and An Garda Síochána
- Ensures follow-up and protection measures are in place
6. Recognising Abuse
Signs include:
- Unexplained injuries
- Withdrawn or fearful behaviour
- Age-inappropriate sexual behaviour or knowledge
- Neglect of hygiene, clothing, food, or supervision
7. Reporting Procedure
Step 1: Staff member informs DLP immediately
Step 2: DLP completes Child Protection and Welfare Report Form
Step 3: Report submitted to Tusla via Portal or directly
Step 4: DLP follows up and records outcomes
In emergency: Contact Gardaí directly
8. Confidentiality
All concerns are handled sensitively and confidentially. Information is shared only on a need-to-know basis in accordance with GDPR and Children First guidelines.
9. Recruitment and Vetting
All staff undergo:
- Garda vetting
- Reference checks
- Induction on child protection policies
10. Training
All staff must complete:
- Children First E-Learning Programme
- Annual refresher training on child safeguarding practices
11. Record Keeping
All concerns, disclosures, and actions taken must be documented and stored securely for 7 years, accessible only to DLP and Manager.

13. Complaints Policy (Summary)
Children and families can make complaints through:
- Verbal or written complaint to staff or manager
- Anonymous form
- Advocate or support person
All complaints must be logged and responded to within 10 working days.
14. Anti-Bullying Policy (Summary)
Zero tolerance approach to bullying:
- Any bullying incidents must be reported to the Key Worker and Manager
- Restorative practice and support plan created
- Safety plan for the victim
Reviewed monthly or after each incident.
15. Behaviour Management Policy (Summary)
Approach includes:
- Positive reinforcement
- Clear and consistent boundaries
- De-escalation strategies
- Use of individual behaviour support plans (BSPs)
No physical punishment permitted under any circumstances.
16. Missing Child Protocol (Summary)
If a child goes missing:
1. Immediate area search by staff
2. Notify Gardaí and parent/guardian
3. Complete Missing Child Report
4. Conduct Return Interview upon recovery
Risk assessments updated after each incident.
17. Staff Code of Conduct (Summary)
Staff must:
- Maintain professional boundaries
- Report safeguarding concerns immediately
- Use respectful, age-appropriate language
- Never engage in private contact with children outside work
18. Data Protection Policy (Summary)
All personal data is:
- Stored securely
- Accessed only on a need-to-know basis
- Retained in accordance with GDPR and Tusla records guidance
Parental/guardian consent required for photography, media, or third-party sharing.
