Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Service User Rights & Advocacy Policy
Section 1: Purpose
1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to affirm and protect the rights of all service users within Secure Living
Support Services Ltd. This includes ensuring individuals are treated with dignity and respect,
supported to make their own decisions, and empowered to raise concerns without fear of
discrimination or reprisal.
This policy also establishes a clear framework for accessing independent advocacy services to
support and represent service users in decisions that affect their lives.

Section 2: Scope
2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals receiving care or support from Secure Living Support Services
Ltd, including residents in HIQA-registered and unregistered settings.
It also applies to all staff members, management, and volunteers who are responsible for upholding
the rights of service users and facilitating access to independent advocacy services.
This policy aligns with the Health Act 2007, the National Standards for Residential Services for
Children and Adults with Disabilities, and relevant human rights and equality legislation.

Section 3: Rights of Residents
3. Rights of Residents
All residents of Secure Living Support Services Ltd have the right to:
- Be treated with dignity, fairness, and respect regardless of age, gender, ability, race, religion, or
background
- Live in a safe and supportive environment free from abuse, neglect, or exploitation
- Be involved in decisions about their care, support, and daily living
- Have their personal privacy and confidentiality upheld
- Access their personal records and request corrections
- Practice their religion and cultural beliefs
- Communicate in their preferred language or method, with support if needed
- Be supported to make complaints or express dissatisfaction without fear
- Access independent advocacy and representation
- Participate in activities and live as independently as possible
- Be consulted about changes in service provision that may affect them
These rights must be actively promoted and respected by all staff.

Section 4: Advocacy Support
4. Advocacy Support
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to ensuring that all residents have access to
independent advocacy to support them in making informed decisions, expressing their views, and
having their rights upheld.
4.1 What is Advocacy:
- Advocacy is a process of supporting individuals to speak up for themselves or be represented by
someone who acts on their behalf
- It is independent, person-centred, and promotes empowerment and inclusion
4.2 Access to Advocacy:
- Residents are informed of their right to advocacy during induction and at regular intervals
- Information about local advocacy services is made available in accessible formats
- Staff will support residents to make contact with advocacy services when requested
4.3 Role of Advocates:
- Advocates may attend meetings, assist with complaints, and support residents in understanding
rights and procedures
- Advocates operate independently and confidentially
4.4 Organisational Responsibility:
- Management must ensure arrangements are in place to facilitate independent advocacy and
remove any barriers to access
- Regular audits will monitor the availability and use of advocacy services

Section 5: Communication
5. Communication
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to ensuring that service users are supported in
communicating their needs, preferences, and concerns in a manner that is respectful, inclusive, and
responsive.
5.1 Individual Communication Needs:
- Each resident's communication preferences and needs will be assessed and documented on
admission
- Support will be provided through accessible information, visual aids, interpreters, or assistive
technology
5.2 Promoting Expression:
- Residents are encouraged and supported to express their views, make choices, and participate in
decision-making
- Staff will use active listening skills and create a safe environment for open dialogue
5.3 Accessible Information:
- All key documents, including policies, service user guides, and complaint procedures, will be
available in formats that meet individual needs (e.g., easy-read, large print, translated)
5.4 Staff Responsibilities:
- Staff must communicate respectfully and clearly at all times
- Training will be provided on communication techniques tailored to individual disabilities and
preferences

Section 6: Informed Consent
6. Informed Consent
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises the fundamental right of all residents to make
informed decisions about their care and support. Staff are responsible for ensuring that consent is
obtained in a respectful and lawful manner.
6.1 Definition:
- Informed consent is a voluntary agreement to a proposed action, made with full knowledge of the
risks, benefits, and alternatives
- It must be given freely, without coercion or undue influence
6.2 Requirements for Consent:
- Information must be provided in a way the resident can understand, using visual aids, interpreters,
or easy-read formats if necessary
- The person must have the capacity to give consent, as defined under the Assisted
Decision-Making (Capacity) Act 2015
- Consent must be recorded and reviewed regularly
6.3 Refusal of Consent:
- Residents have the right to refuse or withdraw consent at any time
- Their decision must be respected, unless it poses a significant risk to their safety or the safety of
others
6.4 Best Interest Decisions:
- If a resident is assessed as lacking capacity, decisions must be made in their best interests with
involvement from family or advocates where appropriate

Section 7: Complaints & Appeals
7. Complaints & Appeals
Secure Living Support Services Ltd ensures that all service users have access to a fair, transparent,
and accessible process for raising concerns, making complaints, and appealing decisions that affect
their care or rights.
7.1 Right to Complain:
- Residents have the right to make complaints without fear of reprisal, discrimination, or
compromised care
- Complaints may be made verbally, in writing, anonymously, or through an advocate
7.2 Procedure:
- All complaints will be acknowledged within 5 working days and resolved promptly where possible
- A designated Complaints Officer will oversee the investigation and communication process
- Records of complaints and outcomes will be securely maintained for audit and learning
7.3 Appeals:
- If a resident is dissatisfied with the outcome of a complaint, they may lodge an appeal with the
senior management team
- Appeals will be reviewed independently and responded to within a clear timeframe
7.4 Advocacy Support:
- Residents will be informed of their right to seek assistance from an independent advocate
throughout the complaints or appeals process
7.5 Learning:
- Themes from complaints and appeals will be reviewed regularly to inform service improvement and
staff training

Section 9: Policy Review
9. Policy Review
This policy will be reviewed on an annual basis or earlier if:
- There are changes in legislation, HIQA standards, or national guidance
- Service user feedback, internal audits, or advocacy reports indicate the need for revision
- Staff or management identify areas for improvement through incident learning or experience
9.1 Review Process:
- The Registered Provider is responsible for coordinating the policy review process
- Revisions will be drafted with input from service users, staff, and advocates
- The updated policy will be approved by the governance board and communicated to all relevant
parties
9.2 Version Control:
- All revisions will be recorded in the version control log and the most recent version made readily
available to staff and residents
