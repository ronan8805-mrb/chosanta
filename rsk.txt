Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Governance Policy - Section 3: Responsibilities
This section outlines the roles and responsibilities of all key governance personnel within Secure
Living Support Services Ltd. These responsibilities are defined to ensure compliance with national
standards and delivery of safe, high-quality care.
1. Registered Provider
- Overall legal responsibility for the centre.
- Ensures compliance with the Health Act 2007 and relevant regulations.
- Approves key appointments, budgets, and operational frameworks.
2. Director (Daisy Cannon)
- Strategic oversight of the organisation's performance and governance.
- Ensures systems for quality improvement and risk management.
- Oversees funding, staffing, and compliance audits.
3. Operational Governance Officer (Ian Devine)
- Leads internal audits and policy enforcement.
- Supports the PIC in implementing service standards.
- Liaises with HIQA and oversees regulatory responses.
4. Person in Charge (PIC)
- Manages daily operations of the designated centre.
- Supervises staff and ensures care planning and delivery.
- Maintains regulatory documentation and leads service quality reviews.
5. Support Staff
- Deliver direct care and support to residents.
- Participate in training, reviews, and staff meetings.
- Adhere to internal policies and promote resident wellbeing.
All roles work collaboratively to ensure governance is transparent, accountable, and focused on
continuous improvement.

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Governance Policy - Section 3: Responsibilities (Audit-Ready)
Secure Living Support Services Ltd ensures that each governance role has a clearly defined scope
of responsibilities, documented and implemented for compliance with HIQA regulations. This section
provides a full breakdown of each role for audit purposes.
1. Registered Provider - Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Legal Entity: Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Primary Role: Ultimate legal accountability and oversight for all residential services.
Audit Detail:
- Maintains up-to-date Statement of Purpose and Governance Structure.
- Ensures a compliant workforce and adequate staffing ratios.
- Oversees all annual reviews, audits, and HIQA inspections.
- Approves all policies and ensures effective governance reporting lines.
- Ensures mandatory notifications (e.g., serious incidents, restrictive practices) are submitted.
2. Director - Daisy Cannon
Position: Director and Strategic Lead
Primary Role: Provides financial and operational oversight.
Audit Detail:
- Leads annual strategic governance meetings.
- Approves quarterly reports from Governance Officer and PIC.
- Reviews audit findings and ensures action plans are implemented.
- Ensures the risk register and service improvement plan are maintained.
- Provides staff resourcing and escalation authority for critical incidents.
3. Operational Governance Officer - Ian Devine
Position: Senior Compliance & Governance Support
Primary Role: Ensure operational procedures align with HIQA standards.
Audit Detail:
- Conducts monthly internal audits and regulatory checks.
- Manages training compliance records and audit trails.
- Liaises with HIQA for inspections and documentation requests.
- Reviews complaints and incident logs and ensures learning is embedded.
- Tracks and reports safeguarding measures and improvement plans.
4. Person in Charge (PIC) - [To Be Appointed]
Position: Centre Manager (HIQA Regulation 14 compliant)
Primary Role: Day-to-day leadership of the designated centre.
Audit Detail:
- Ensures all care plans, assessments, and behavioural support strategies are current.
- Oversees recruitment, rosters, supervision records, and induction documentation.
- Maintains daily logs, medication records, incident reports, and personal planning files.
- Conducts team meetings, shift handovers, and daily checklists.
- Reports to Governance Officer weekly and escalates critical risks immediately.
5. Multidisciplinary & Support Staff
Position: Frontline Care Staff
Primary Role: Deliver daily care and support for residents.
Audit Detail:
- Follow agreed personal plans and document interventions.
- Complete daily notes, food/fluid charts, incident logs, and medical observation forms.
- Attend mandatory training (e.g., Fire Safety, MAPA, First Aid, Infection Control).
- Receive regular supervision and performance reviews.
- Adhere to safeguarding policy and report concerns through designated channels.
Together, these defined responsibilities create a robust governance framework that satisfies HIQA
Regulation 23 (Governance and Management) and ensures safe, accountable, and person-centred
service delivery.

First Aid:
The greatest element of risk for a First Aid Responder is transfer of the virus to the mucous membranes by contact of contaminated hands (including contaminated gloved hands) with the eyes, nose or mouth. 
 
The key interventions to manage this risk are to minimise hand contamination, avoid touching your face and clean your hands frequently with soap and water or alcohol-based hand gel.
Wear the relevant PPE as outlined by your dynamic Risk Assessment
Any person presenting with symptoms consistent with COVID-19 should be treated as a suspected case and move them to an isolated room
One First Aider to treat the person 
Additional PPE (enclosed eye protection and FFP3 mask if available) should be worn by First Aid Responders when responding to all first aid incidents where close contact cannot be avoided. Please also have a mask available to give to person if they are displaying symptoms consistent with COVID-19 to limit droplet dispersion.
If you suspect a person has experienced a cardiac arrest, do not listen or feel for breathing by placing your ear and cheek close to the person’s mouth. If you are in any doubt about confirming cardiac arrest, the default position is to start chest compressions only until help arrives. To iterate the point, a person in cardiac arrest should have compression only CPR applied. 
Persons with minor injuries (cuts, abrasions, minor burns) - where practical, a First Aid Responder should avoid close contact and advise the injured party what steps to take in treating their injury. 
No reusable equipment should be returned to service without being cleaned/disinfected appropriately.
The following PPE must be available for responding to first aid incidents: 
1. Disposable gloves (nitrile/latex) 
2. FFP3 or FFP2 Face masks 
3. Disposable plastic aprons
4. Enclosed eye protection First Aid Responder must ensure that the mask covers both the mouth and nose and is fitted correctly to create an adequate seal to the face.

Workers returning after COVID19 Suspected & Confirmed Cases:
An individual must only return to work if deemed fit to do so and upon approval of their medical advisor and having coordinated with their manager/designated HR/employer contact. When an individual is symptom-free and are deemed fit to return to work, the key criteria are: 
14 days since their last “close contact” with a confirmed/suspected case and have not developed symptoms in that time, or 
14 days since the onset of their symptoms and 5 days since their last fever (high temperature), or 
They have been advised by a GP / healthcare provider to return to work
Individual must self-declare their fitness for work in the absence of having a fitness for work certificate from their GP/healthcare provider. This is in acknowledgement that GP’s don’t currently have capacity to be issuing return to work certificates
 
 
Cleaning of Areas after Suspected/Confirmed Contamination: 
It is recommended cleaning an area with normal household disinfectant after a suspected coronavirus (COVID-19) case has left will reduce the risk of passing the infection on to other people
If an area can be kept closed and secure for 72 hours, wait until this time has passed for cleaning as the amount of virus living on surfaces will have reduced in 72 hours 
For cleaning purposes, wear a face mask, disposable or washing up gloves. These should be double-bagged, then stored securely for 72 hours then thrown away in the regular rubbish
If an area has been heavily contaminated, such as with visible bodily fluids, from a person with coronavirus (COVID-19), consider using protection for the eyes, mouth and nose, as well as wearing gloves and an apron
Wash hands regularly with soap and water for 20 seconds, and after removing gloves, aprons and other protection used while cleaning
Use disposable cloths or paper roll and disposable mop heads, to clean all hard surfaces, floors, chairs, door handles and sanitary fittings
Waste from possible cases and cleaning of areas where possible cases have been (including disposable cloths and tissues): 
Should be put in a plastic rubbish bag and tied when full. 
The plastic bag should then be placed in a second bin bag and tied. 
It should be put in a suitable and secure place and marked for storage until the individual’s test results are known.

Workers returning after COVID19 Suspected & Confirmed Cases:
An individual must only return to work if deemed fit to do so and upon approval of their medical advisor and having coordinated with their manager/designated HR/employer contact. When an individual is symptom-free and are deemed fit to return to work, the key criteria are: 
14 days since their last “close contact” with a confirmed/suspected case and have not developed symptoms in that time, or 
14 days since the onset of their symptoms and 5 days since their last fever (high temperature), or 
They have been advised by a GP / healthcare provider to return to work
Individual must self-declare their fitness for work in the absence of having a fitness for work certificate from their GP/healthcare provider. This is in acknowledgement that GP’s don’t currently have capacity to be issuing return to work certificates
 
 
Cleaning of Areas after Suspected/Confirmed Contamination: 
It is recommended cleaning an area with normal household disinfectant after a suspected coronavirus (COVID-19) case has left will reduce the risk of passing the infection on to other people
If an area can be kept closed and secure for 72 hours, wait until this time has passed for cleaning as the amount of virus living on surfaces will have reduced in 72 hours 
For cleaning purposes, wear a face mask, disposable or washing up gloves. These should be double-bagged, then stored securely for 72 hours then thrown away in the regular rubbish
If an area has been heavily contaminated, such as with visible bodily fluids, from a person with coronavirus (COVID-19), consider using protection for the eyes, mouth and nose, as well as wearing gloves and an apron
Wash hands regularly with soap and water for 20 seconds, and after removing gloves, aprons and other protection used while cleaning
Use disposable cloths or paper roll and disposable mop heads, to clean all hard surfaces, floors, chairs, door handles and sanitary fittings
Waste from possible cases and cleaning of areas where possible cases have been (including disposable cloths and tissues): 
Should be put in a plastic rubbish bag and tied when full. 
The plastic bag should then be placed in a second bin bag and tied. 
It should be put in a suitable and secure place and marked for storage until the individual’s test results are known.
