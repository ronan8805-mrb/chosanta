Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy - Section 1: Purpose
1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all staff employed by Secure Living Support Services Ltd
are recruited, vetted, and inducted in a safe, consistent, and legally compliant manner.
This policy is designed to:
- Protect vulnerable adults and children by ensuring all staff are appropriately vetted before
employment
- Promote transparent and fair recruitment practices that align with employment law and best
practice in health and social care
- Ensure all new staff are fully inducted into the values, procedures, and expectations of the
organisation before undertaking duties
- Support the creation of a competent, safe, and effective workforce that enhances the quality of life
and safety of all residents

=====Staff_Recruitment_Policy_Section2_Scope.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy - Section 2: Scope
2. Scope
This policy applies to all individuals who are employed, engaged, or placed within any service
operated by Secure Living Support Services Ltd, including:
- Full-time and part-time employees
- Relief and agency staff
- Volunteers and student placements
- Contractors or third-party service providers working directly with residents
- Management and HR staff involved in recruitment, vetting, and onboarding processes
This scope ensures that anyone working directly or indirectly with vulnerable individuals within our
services is subject to the same robust recruitment, vetting, and induction standards, in line with
HIQA regulations and safeguarding best practice.

=====Staff_Recruitment_Policy_Section3_Recruitment_Principles.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy - Section 3: Recruitment Principles
3. Recruitment Principles
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to a fair, transparent, and values-driven
recruitment process. The following principles guide all recruitment activities:
3.1 Equal Opportunities
- Recruitment decisions are made based on merit, qualifications, and suitability for the role.
- No applicant will be discriminated against on the basis of age, gender, sexual orientation, ethnicity,
religion, disability, or background.
3.2 Role Clarity
- Each vacancy will have a clearly defined job description, person specification, and core
responsibilities.
- Role advertisements will outline:
  - Essential qualifications or experience
  - Safeguarding requirements
  - Registration with professional bodies (if applicable)
3.3 Transparent Process
- A structured recruitment process will be followed, including:
  - Application shortlisting
  - Structured interviews
  - Competency-based assessments (where relevant)
  - Reference and vetting checks
3.4 Safeguarding Priority
- All roles are treated as positions of trust. As such:
  - Safeguarding awareness is a core part of selection criteria
  - Any concerns during recruitment are escalated immediately
  - Pre-employment checks are non-negotiable for any direct care role

=====Staff_Recruitment_Policy_Section4_Selection_Process.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy - Section 4: Selection Process
4. Selection Process
Secure Living Support Services Ltd follows a structured and fair selection process to ensure that
only qualified, suitable, and values-aligned individuals are appointed to roles supporting vulnerable
individuals.
4.1 Shortlisting
- All applications are reviewed against the job description and person specification.
- Shortlisting is carried out by at least two senior staff members to ensure objectivity and fairness.
- Applicants with incomplete forms or missing qualifications will not progress unless clarified.
4.2 Interview
- Interviews are conducted by a panel, ideally consisting of at least two people:
  - One operational/clinical lead
  - One HR or managerial representative
- All candidates are asked the same core competency-based questions focused on:
  - Values and attitudes
  - Understanding of safeguarding
  - Professional experience and skills
  - Conflict management and teamwork
4.3 Assessment
- For some roles (e.g. clinical or care positions), additional assessments may be included such as:
  - Role-specific scenario tasks
  - Written exercises or presentations
  - Shadowing or observational tasks (with consent)
4.4 Decision and Offer
- Selection is based on overall merit, including:
  - Interview performance
  - Alignment with organisational values
  - Reference and vetting outcomes
- The chosen candidate is issued a conditional offer pending:
  - Garda vetting
  - Reference checks
  - Verification of qualifications

Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy
Section 5: Garda Vetting
Garda Vetting is a legal and regulatory requirement for all individuals working with vulnerable
persons. Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to ensuring that no staff member begins
work without appropriate vetting clearance.
5.1 Legal Compliance
- All Garda Vetting is processed under the National Vetting Bureau (Children and Vulnerable
Persons) Acts 2012 to 2016.
- Secure Living Support Services Ltd is registered with an authorised Garda Vetting organisation.
5.2 Mandatory Requirement
- No staff member, volunteer, or contractor will be permitted to work directly with residents without
completed vetting clearance.
- In exceptional cases, conditional supervised duties may be approved only with senior management
authorisation, pending full vetting.
5.3 Re-Vetting
- Garda Vetting is renewed every 3 years or earlier if:
  - The staff member changes role
  - A safeguarding concern arises
  - There is a significant break in service
5.4 Handling Disclosures
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy
- All vetting disclosures are reviewed by senior management.
- Where disclosures exist:
  - The individual is informed confidentially
  - A risk assessment is conducted before a decision on employment is made
  - Decisions are documented with reference to safeguarding duties
5.5 Record Keeping
- Copies of all vetting disclosures are securely stored in each staff member's confidential personnel
file, in line with data protection legislation.

=====Staff_Recruitment_Policy_Section6_Contracts_Declarations.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy - Section 6: Contracts and Declarations
6. Contracts and Declarations
Secure Living Support Services Ltd ensures that all staff understand and formally agree to the
terms, responsibilities, and conduct expected of them before commencing their duties.
6.1 Written Contracts of Employment
- Every staff member receives a written contract outlining:
  - Terms and conditions of employment
  - Working hours and salary
  - Probationary period
  - Role description and reporting lines
  - Grievance and disciplinary procedures
- Contracts are signed and returned before the first day of employment.
6.2 Code of Conduct Declaration
- All staff must sign a declaration confirming their understanding of:
  - The organisation's Code of Conduct
  - Respect for residents' rights and dignity
  - Confidentiality obligations
  - Zero tolerance for abuse, neglect, or discrimination
6.3 Declaration of Health & Character
- Staff must complete a declaration confirming:
  - They are fit to work in a care environment
  - They have no outstanding charges or convictions that would pose a risk
  - They are willing to undergo occupational health checks if required
6.4 Data Protection and Confidentiality Agreement
- All staff sign a GDPR-compliant confidentiality agreement.
- This agreement ensures understanding of:
  - How personal data is stored and accessed
  - The importance of safeguarding resident records and private information
  - Consequences of breaches

=====Staff_Recruitment_Policy_Section7_Induction_Programme.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy - Section 7: Induction Programme
7. Induction Programme
Secure Living Support Services Ltd provides a structured and comprehensive induction programme
to ensure that all new staff are fully prepared to deliver high-quality, person-centred care from the
outset of their employment.
7.1 Purpose of Induction
- To ensure that new staff understand the organisation's mission, values, and care standards
- To familiarise staff with:
  - Policies and procedures
  - Resident needs and rights
  - Safeguarding protocols
  - Emergency and health & safety procedures
7.2 Core Components
The induction process includes the following key elements:
- Organisational Overview
  - Vision, mission, and values of Secure Living Support Services Ltd
  - Structure and governance
- Service-Specific Orientation
  - Introduction to the care setting
  - Resident profiles and communication needs
  - HIQA compliance requirements
- Policy & Procedure Training
  - Safeguarding Vulnerable Persons
  - Medication Management
  - Incident Reporting
  - Fire Safety & Evacuation Plans
- Role-Specific Training
  - Job duties and supervision
  - Manual handling and infection control
  - Health & safety protocols
7.3 Supervised Practice
- All new staff complete a minimum 5-day supervised induction period before working independently.
- A senior staff member signs off on core competencies before duties are assigned.
7.4 Record Keeping
- An induction checklist is signed by both the staff member and supervisor.
- Records are kept on the staff file for audit and compliance verification.

=====Staff_Recruitment_Policy_Section8_Probation_Supervision.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy - Section 8: Probation and Ongoing Supervision
8. Probation and Ongoing Supervision
Secure Living Support Services Ltd implements a robust probation and supervision process to
ensure staff are supported, monitored, and continuously improving in their roles.
8.1 Probation Period
- All new staff undergo a minimum 6-month probation period, unless otherwise stated in the contract.
- During probation, staff performance, conduct, and attitude are closely monitored through:
  - Regular supervision meetings (monthly)
  - Feedback from residents and senior staff
  - Review of incident logs, punctuality, and adherence to policies
8.2 Probation Review
- At the end of the probation period, a formal Probation Review Meeting is held.
- Outcomes of the review may include:
  - Confirmation of permanent employment
  - Extension of probation (with clear goals)
  - Termination if the role is not a good fit
- The review is documented and signed by both parties.
8.3 Supervision
- Staff receive structured one-to-one supervision at least every 6-8 weeks.
- Supervision includes discussion of:
  - Resident needs and challenges
  - Staff wellbeing and workload
  - Training or development goals
  - Safeguarding, incidents, and communication
8.4 Record Keeping
- All supervision and probation records are stored securely in the staff personnel file.
- Supervision records contribute to performance management, audit readiness, and HIQA
compliance.

=====Staff_Recruitment_Policy_Section9_Training_Register.pdf=====
Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Staff Recruitment, Vetting, and Induction Policy - Section 9: Training Register
9. Training Register
Secure Living Support Services Ltd maintains a centralised Training Register to ensure all staff
receive appropriate, up-to-date training in accordance with legal requirements, HIQA standards, and
the needs of residents.
9.1 Purpose
- To document all mandatory and supplementary training completed by each staff member
- To track renewal dates, ensure compliance, and identify training gaps
- To support staff development and organisational learning
9.2 Contents of the Register
Each staff member's record includes:
- Full name and job title
- Start date
- All training completed (with provider and date)
- Expiry/renewal date of each training
- Notes on refresher needs or additional requirements
- Supervisor sign-off
9.3 Mandatory Training
The following courses must be recorded for all staff:
- Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults and Children
- Manual Handling & Patient Moving
- Fire Safety
- Infection Prevention and Control
- First Aid / CPR
- Medication Management (if applicable)
- GDPR and Data Protection
- HIQA Standards & Regulations
9.4 Access and Updates
- The register is maintained by the HR or Compliance Officer.
- It is reviewed monthly and updated following every training event.
- It is accessible to management for audits, SLA reviews, and HIQA inspections.
