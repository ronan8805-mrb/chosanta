Intimate Care Policy

Sections 1–3: Purpose, Scope & Principles

1 Purpose

This policy ensures that all intimate care provided to residents of Chosanta Management is delivered in a manner that protects dignity, privacy, independence, and wellbeing.

  • Personal hygiene
  • Continence care
  • Dressing and undressing
  • Menstrual care
  • Support with eating or drinking if required
2 Scope
  • All staff involved in providing or supporting residents with any form of intimate care
  • Residents of all genders and ages with personal care needs
  • One-to-one and assisted care delivery; use of continence aids and bathing equipment
  • Respecting gender, cultural, and religious sensitivities
  • Situations involving consent, risk, and protection from abuse
3 Principles of Intimate Care
  • Dignity & Respect: Every resident has the right to privacy and dignity at all times
  • Consent & Choice: Care only provided with consent; residents supported to express preferences
  • Independence: Residents encouraged to carry out aspects of care themselves where possible
  • Safety: Practices follow infection control standards and are risk-assessed
  • Communication: Staff explain procedures and ensure residents are comfortable and informed
  • Gender Sensitivity: Resident requests regarding carer gender respected where feasible
  • Professionalism: Staff conduct with discretion, empathy, and awareness of boundaries

Intimate Care Policy

Sections 4–6: Consent, Staff Practice & Privacy

4 Consent
  • Verbal or non-verbal consent sought before each instance of intimate care
  • Resident's right to refuse or withdraw consent respected at any time
  • Individuals supported to communicate consent in their preferred method (gestures, signs, assistive devices)
  • Where capacity is in question, formal capacity assessment carried out under the Assisted Decision-Making (Capacity) Act 2015
  • Consent documented clearly in personal care plans and reviewed regularly
5 Staff Practice
  • Explain each step and check consent before proceeding
  • Ensure privacy — close doors, use screens, limit exposure
  • Use gloves, aprons, and appropriate PPE for hygiene
  • Use soft tone, calm manner, and respectful language
  • Encourage independence; never rush or pressure a resident
  • Report any unusual findings (bruising, skin changes) immediately
  • Document care provided clearly and factually
  • Maintain professional boundaries at all times
6 Privacy & Dignity
  • Care provided in private spaces with minimal interruptions
  • Knock before entering and explain intentions clearly
  • Cover the resident appropriately; expose only what is necessary
  • Allow residents time to respond, participate, or ask questions
  • Accommodate cultural and personal preferences regarding gender and routines

Sections 7–10: Records, Safeguarding, Training & Review

7 Record Keeping
  • Record each instance of intimate care in daily care notes — time, nature, and observations
  • Report any concerns, injuries, refusals, or unusual findings to PIC immediately
  • Use objective, factual, and respectful language in all entries
  • Records stored securely; accessible only to authorised personnel (GDPR & HIQA compliant)
8 Safeguarding & Reporting Concerns
  • Report concerns, disclosures, or suspicions immediately to the DLP or PIC
  • Document concerns factually and confidentially
  • Follow safeguarding policy and Children First / National Safeguarding guidelines
  • Protect resident from further harm while concern is being addressed
  • Cooperate fully with investigations; ensure resident has access to advocacy and support
9 Training
  • Intimate care procedures and personal care planning
  • Infection prevention and control
  • Safeguarding vulnerable adults and children
  • Consent, dignity, and communication in care
  • Recognising and responding to distress, refusal, or changes in behaviour
  • Refresher training provided annually or as required
10 Policy Review
  • Reviewed annually or sooner if changes in legislation, HIQA standards, or practice
  • Feedback from residents, staff, and advocates incorporated
  • Review of incident reports and practice audits informs revisions
  • Oversight by the Registered Provider and Person in Charge
  • Revised versions communicated to staff and made available in appropriate formats
