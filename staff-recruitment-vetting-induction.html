Staff Recruitment, Vetting & Induction

Comprehensive Policy — HIQA Compliant

1 Purpose

To ensure all staff are recruited, vetted, and inducted in a safe, consistent, and legally compliant manner — protecting vulnerable adults and children, promoting transparent practices, and creating a competent workforce.

2 Scope
  • Full-time, part-time, relief, and agency staff; volunteers and student placements
  • Contractors or third-party providers with direct/indirect resident contact
  • Management and HR staff involved in recruitment and onboarding
3 Recruitment Principles
  • Equal Opportunities: Decisions on merit; no discrimination on age, gender, orientation, ethnicity, religion, disability
  • Role Clarity: Defined job description, person spec, essential qualifications, safeguarding requirements
  • Transparent Process: Shortlisting → structured interviews → competency assessment → reference/vetting checks
  • Safeguarding Priority: All roles treated as positions of trust; concerns escalated immediately
4 Selection Process
  • Shortlisting: By ≥2 senior staff against job description; incomplete applications do not progress
  • Interview: Panel of ≥2 (operational lead + HR); competency-based questions on values, safeguarding, experience, teamwork
  • Assessment: Scenario tasks, written exercises, or shadowing for clinical/care roles
  • Conditional offer pending: Garda vetting, reference checks, qualification verification

Garda Vetting, Contracts & Induction Programme

5 Garda Vetting
  • Processed under National Vetting Bureau (Children and Vulnerable Persons) Acts 2012–2016
  • Mandatory: No staff/volunteer/contractor works with residents without completed clearance
  • Exceptional conditional supervised duties only with senior management authorisation
  • Re-vetting: Every 3 years, or on role change, safeguarding concern, or service break
  • Disclosures reviewed by senior management; risk assessment before employment decision; securely stored
6 Contracts & Declarations
  • Written contract: Terms, hours, salary, probation, role description, grievance/disciplinary procedures — signed before first day
  • Code of Conduct: Declaration confirming understanding of residents' rights, confidentiality, zero tolerance for abuse
  • Health & Character: Declaration of fitness to work, no outstanding charges posing risk
  • GDPR: Confidentiality agreement covering data storage, access, and breach consequences
7 Induction Programme
  • Organisational: Vision, mission, values, structure, governance
  • Service-specific: Care setting, resident profiles, communication needs, HIQA compliance
  • Policy training: Safeguarding, Medication Management, Incident Reporting, Fire Safety & Evacuation
  • Role-specific: Job duties, supervision, manual handling, infection control, H&S
  • Supervised practice: Minimum 5-day period; senior staff signs off core competencies before independent duties
  • Induction checklist signed by staff member and supervisor; retained on file for audit

8 Probation & Supervision

  • Probation review: Formal meeting — confirm permanent, extend with goals, or terminate; documented and signed
  • Ongoing supervision: 1:1 every 6–8 weeks covering resident needs, staff wellbeing, training goals, safeguarding
