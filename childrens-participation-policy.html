Children's Participation & Rights Policy

HIQA National Standards 2018 — Standards 1 & 2 · UN Convention on the Rights of the Child

Chosanta Management is committed to ensuring that every child and young person in our care has a genuine, meaningful voice in decisions affecting their life and care. This is not a paper exercise — it is a lived reality underpinning everything we do.

Legislative & Regulatory Basis: UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC) · Child Care Act 1991 · Children First Act 2015 · HIQA National Standards for Children's Residential Centres 2018, Standard 1 (Children's Rights) and Standard 2 (Children's Needs) · Tusla Child and Youth Participation guidance

1 Core Principles
  • Voice: Every child has the right to express their views, feelings, and wishes — and to have these taken seriously in all decisions affecting them
  • Influence: Children's views must demonstrably influence decisions about their care — not just be recorded and ignored
  • Access to advocacy: Every child must know they can access an independent advocate and must be given information about how to do so at admission and at every care plan review
  • Accessible complaints: The complaints process must be explained in plain language at admission and displayed visibly in the home
  • Privacy & dignity: Every child has the right to privacy, to be treated with dignity, and to have their cultural, religious, and personal identity respected
  • Right to refuse: Children have the right to say no to participation activities — participation must never be coerced
