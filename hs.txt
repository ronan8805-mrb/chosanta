Secure Living Support Services Ltd
Health & Safety Policy
Section 1: Purpose
1. Purpose
The purpose of this Health & Safety Policy is to establish and maintain a safe and healthy
environment for all service users, staff, visitors, and contractors at Secure Living Support Services
Ltd.
This policy ensures compliance with the Safety, Health and Welfare at Work Act 2005 and relevant
HIQA standards. It outlines the organisation's commitment to preventing injury and ill health,
promoting wellbeing, and ensuring that all health and safety risks are assessed and managed
effectively.

2. Scope
This Health & Safety Policy applies to:

Section 3: Responsibilities
3. Responsibilities
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises that effective health and safety management relies
on clear and shared responsibilities.
3.1 Management:
- Ensure compliance with health and safety legislation and best practice
- Provide appropriate resources for health and safety, including training and protective equipment
- Conduct risk assessments and ensure safety controls are implemented
- Investigate incidents and take corrective action where necessary
3.2 Staff:
- Take reasonable care for their own health and safety and that of others
- Follow all safety protocols and procedures
- Report hazards, near misses, or accidents promptly
- Participate in required health and safety training
3.3 Service Users:
- Are encouraged to follow house rules that promote safety
- Are supported to understand and participate in safety measures relevant to their environment

Section 4: Risk Assessment and Hazard Control
4. Risk Assessment and Hazard Control
Secure Living Support Services Ltd carries out regular and systematic risk assessments to identify,
evaluate, and manage potential hazards that may affect staff, service users, visitors, or contractors.
All risk assessments are documented and reviewed regularly, especially following incidents or
changes in operations.
Control measures are put in place to eliminate or reduce risks and include:
- Use of personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Staff training and supervision
- Environmental controls and safety signage
- Emergency preparedness and planning
Staff are required to report any new risks or hazards immediately to the Person in Charge, who will
update assessments and implement additional controls as needed.

Section 5: Accident & Incident Reporting
5. Accident & Incident Reporting
All accidents, near misses, and incidents must be reported and documented in a timely and accurate
manner to ensure legal compliance, ongoing safety improvements, and risk reduction.
Procedures include:
- Immediate reporting to the Person in Charge or designated safety officer
- Completion of the Incident Report Form within 24 hours
- Prompt medical attention where required
- Investigation of root causes and contributing factors
- Implementation of corrective and preventative measures
Serious incidents will be reported to external authorities (e.g., HIQA, HSA, or Tusla) in line with legal
requirements. All reports are reviewed and stored securely for learning, trend analysis, and audit
purposes.

Section 6: Fire Safety
6. Fire Safety
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to ensuring the highest standards of fire safety
across all facilities. Fire safety is managed in compliance with relevant fire regulations, HIQA
requirements, and local authority guidelines.
Key measures include:
- Regular fire safety risk assessments
- Maintenance and inspection of fire detection and alarm systems
- Clearly marked fire exits and escape routes
- Fire extinguishers placed in accessible areas and serviced regularly
- Emergency lighting in corridors and communal spaces
- Display of evacuation plans in key locations
- Training for all staff in fire prevention, evacuation procedures, and use of extinguishers
Fire drills are conducted at least quarterly, and Personal Emergency Evacuation Plans (PEEPs) are
in place for all service users requiring assistance during evacuation.

Section 7: Manual Handling & Ergonomics
7. Manual Handling & Ergonomics
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises the risks associated with manual handling and poor
ergonomic practices. All staff involved in physical activities, such as lifting, supporting residents, or
moving equipment, must be trained in safe manual handling techniques.
The organisation ensures:
- Completion of Manual Handling and Patient Handling training for all relevant staff
- Use of mechanical aids (e.g., hoists, trolleys) to minimise strain
- Proper posture and lifting techniques are promoted through ongoing training
- Workstations and equipment are set up to reduce musculoskeletal strain
- Regular reviews of ergonomic practices to ensure compliance and comfort
Managers are responsible for supervising manual handling activities and supporting staff to work
safely and efficiently.

Section 8: Infection Control
8. Infection Control
To safeguard the health and wellbeing of service users and staff, Secure Living Support Services
Ltd implements robust infection prevention and control procedures. These procedures align with
HSE and HIQA standards and are based on best practice.
Measures include:
- Routine hand hygiene and use of hand sanitiser
- Appropriate use and disposal of personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Regular cleaning and disinfection of high-touch surfaces
- Isolation protocols for individuals showing symptoms of infectious conditions
- Safe handling of waste, laundry, and sharps
- Staff training in standard and transmission-based precautions
All infection control procedures are reviewed regularly, and outbreak response plans are in place for
rapid containment if needed.

Section 9: Mental Health & Wellbeing
9. Mental Health & Wellbeing
Secure Living Support Services Ltd recognises the importance of mental health and emotional
wellbeing in a safe and healthy workplace. The organisation is committed to promoting a supportive
environment where staff and service users can thrive.
Our commitments include:
- Promoting open communication and a culture of mutual respect
- Providing access to Employee Assistance Programmes (EAPs) or counselling support
- Encouraging work-life balance and stress-reducing practices
- Identifying and reducing psychosocial risks in the workplace
- Providing mental health training to staff to recognise signs of distress in themselves or others
Mental wellbeing is integrated into health and safety planning and supported through regular staff
supervision and feedback mechanisms.

Section 10: Monitoring & Audit
10. Monitoring & Audit
Secure Living Support Services Ltd is committed to continuously improving health and safety
performance through regular monitoring, audits, and evaluations.
Key practices include:
- Scheduled internal audits of health and safety systems
- Monthly inspections by management or health & safety representatives
- Incident reviews and analysis to identify trends or recurring risks
- Service user and staff feedback on safety conditions
- Action plans developed and tracked to address audit findings
All audit reports are retained and reviewed by senior management. Findings are used to revise
policies, update training, and strengthen the safety culture within the organisation.

Section 11: Policy Review
11. Policy Review
This Health & Safety Policy is reviewed annually or more frequently if there are significant changes
in operations, regulations, or following an incident that requires policy revision.
The Person in Charge and senior management are responsible for:
- Ensuring the policy remains compliant with relevant legislation and standards
- Consulting with staff and service users on safety issues and incorporating feedback
- Updating risk assessments, training materials, and procedures as needed
Review dates and version control are documented clearly, and all staff are informed of changes to
ensure consistent understanding and implementation.
